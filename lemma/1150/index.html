androclas

Latin noun
quoted from Greek

Last edited: 12/1/2023, 3:42:57 PM
Primary meaning the 63rd year
Literal translation man-breaking
Variants
androclas
Edited by
Kierán Meinhardt
the 63rd year astrological concept

Hac ex causa ab Aegyptiis androclas dictus est, quod omnem viri substantiam frangat ac debilitet.

‘Therefore this year is called ἀνδροκλάς by the Egyptians, because it breaks and debilitates all substance of life.’

— Firmicus Maternus, Mathesis 4.20.3

Genre: astrological compendium
Provenance: Sicily
Date: ca. 334 CE

Firmicus Maternus. 1897. Ivlii Firmici Materni Matheseos Libri VIII, ed. W. Kroll, F. Skutsch. Stuttgart: B. G. Teubner. 1.258
