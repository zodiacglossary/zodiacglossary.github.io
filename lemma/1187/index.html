asicat

Latin noun
loaned from Egyptian

Last edited: 10/7/2025, 8:19:31 AM
Primary meaning 4°–10° Taurus
Variants
asicat
Edited by
Kierán Meinhardt
4°–10° Taurus decan

In Tauri signo quae sint partes plenae, quae vacuae, hoc demonstratur exemplo. Sunt autem in Tauro plenae partes XX, vacuae partes X. I locus partes III vacuus II locus partes VII Suo [idest asicat] III locus partes II vacuus IV locus partes VIII Aryo [idest ason/asou] V locus partes V vacuus VI locus partes V Romanae [idest aarfi/arfa]

‘In the sign of Taurus, some degrees are full, some are empty. This will be shown by example. But in Taurus there are 20° full and 10° empty. 1. 3° empty 2. 7° Suo [i.e. Asicat] 3. 2° empty 4. 8° Aryo [i.e. Ason/Asou] 5. 5° empty 6. 5° Romanae [i.e. Aarfi/Arfa]’

— Firmicus Maternus, Mathesis 4.22.9

Genre: astrological compendium
Provenance: Sicily
Date: ca. 334 CE

Firmicus Maternus. 1897. Ivlii Firmici Materni Matheseos Libri VIII, ed. W. Kroll, F. Skutsch. Stuttgart: B. G. Teubner. 1.267
