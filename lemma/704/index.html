TE
ṭeḫû

Akkadian verb

Last edited: 6/10/2024, 8:03:05 AM
Primary meaning to come close
Literal translation to come close
Variants
TE ṭeḫû
Edited by
Alessia Pilloni
to come close, to approach verb of motion

[... ina] ⸢ZALAG₂⸣ dele-bat e GIR₃-ar₂-ša₂-UR.A 1 SI TE GE₆ 17 ina ZALAG₂ ⸢sin⸣ [... ŠUR-GIGIR-ša₂ ...]

‘[... la]st part of the night, Venus was 1 finger above β Virginis, it came close. Night of the 17th, last part of the night, the moo[n was ... β/ζ Tauri ...]’

— BM 45766 r. 7'

Genre: astronomical diary
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 323 BC

Sachs, A. J., Hunger, H. 1988. Astronomical Diaries and Related Texts from Babylonia, Vol. I: Diaries from 652 B.C. to 262 B.C. Vienna: Verlag der Österreichischen Akademie der Wissenschaften. 206-207
