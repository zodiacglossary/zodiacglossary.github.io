from preposition

a point in space and time

igi ana igi ta 25 maš.maš en 30 gir2.tab 30 tab

‘(First) appearance to (first) appearance. From 25 Gem until 30 Sco you add 30.’

— BM 34081+ (BMAPT No. 18) obv. i 8'

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 250-50 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer. 248

sin TA SIG x pa-nu-šu2 ana MURU4 GAR.MEŠ

‘The moon goes from the distance below the ecliptic toward the node’

— W 20030/10 obv. 9

Genre: horoscope
Provenance: Uruk
Date: Seleucid

Rochberg, F. 1998. Babylonian Horoscopes. Transactions of the American Philosophical Society, 88, 1–164. 100-101

"SIG" was previously interpreted as "negative latitude". This is misleading, since in Babylonian there is no concept of "negative numbers".
