simul collocatus

Latin adjective

Last edited: 1/12/2024, 3:48:32 PM
Primary meaning in conjunction
Literal translation placed at once
Variants
simul collocatus
Edited by
Kierán Meinhardt
in conjunction aspect

presumably broadly, i.e. by sign

Quodsi simul Mercurium ac Venerem collocates malignum sidus Martis aspexerit, faciles nuptias sed cum quadam infamia ac nota decernunt.

‘If Mercury and Venus are in conjunction and the malefic planet Mars aspects them, they decree easy but scandalous marriages’

— Firmicus Maternus, Mathesis 6.26.3

Genre: astrological compendium
Provenance: Sicily
Date: ca. 334 CE

Firmicus Maternus. 1897. Ivlii Firmici Materni Matheseos Libri VIII, ed. W. Kroll, F. Skutsch. Stuttgart: B. G. Teubner. 1.130
