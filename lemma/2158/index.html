simplex

Latin adjective

Last edited: 9/23/2024, 8:03:33 AM
Primary meaning single-bodied
Literal translation single
Variants
simplex
Edited by
Kierán Meinhardt
single-bodied astrological concept, zodiac sign quality

signs which are not double-bodied, i.e. cardinal or fixed

sic et simplicibus signis stat forma quadrata; nam neque Taurus habet comitem, nec iungitur ulli horrendus Leo, nec metuit sine compare quemquam Scorpios, atque uno censetur Aquarius astro.

‘Likewise the single signs also enjoy the figure of a square; for the Bull has no partner, nor has the fierce Lion another at his side; the mateless Scorpion has no one to fear, and the Waterman is counted as of single sign.’

— Manilius, Astronomica 2.664 ff

Genre: astrological treatise

Manilius. Astronomica. Edited and translated by G. P. Goold. Loeb Classical Library 469. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press, 1977. 134-135
