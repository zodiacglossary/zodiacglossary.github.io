ṣal-mu
ṣalmu

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 6/26/2023, 6:43:26 PM
Primary meaning human figure
Literal translation statue
Variants
ṣal-mu ṣalmu
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
human figure constellations: descriptive term

(8) DIŠ mul.MAŠ.TAB.BA.T[UR.TUR] 2 ṣal-mu lu-[b]u-uš-tu4 ziq-na zaq-nu (9) 2 MUL.MEŠ ina [SAG-šú-nu] eṣ-ru ṣal-mu IGI-ú ina ŠU ZÀG-šú il-tuh-a na-ši

‘(8) ¶ The L[esser] Twins are two clothed human figures with beards, provided with a kurkurru. (9) 2 stars are drawn [at their heads]. The front figure carries a whip in his right hand.’

— VAT 9428 obv. 8-9

Genre: uranology text
Provenance: Assur

P.-A. Beaulieu, E. Frahm, W. Horowitz and J. Steele 2018, The Cuneiform Uranology Texts. Drawing the Constellations. Transactions of the American Philosophical Society 107, Part 2 22, 24
