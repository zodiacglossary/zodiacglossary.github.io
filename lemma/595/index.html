tmws

Egyptian noun
loaned from Aramaic

Last edited: 11/3/2024, 4:03:40 PM
Primary meaning Tammus (month IV)
Variants
tmws*
Edited by
Andreas Winkler
Tammus (month IV) lunar month, time interval

Originally Babylonian month name in Egyptian transcription through an Aramaic source

[nysn pꜣ wrš] | ỉbd-4 ꜣḫ.t ꜣyr tpy [pr.t pꜣy šywn pꜣ wrš ỉbd-2 pr.t pꜣy tmws pꜣ] | wrš ỉbd-3 pr.t pꜣy

‘[Nisan is lunar month] | 4 of Akhet; Iyar is the first month of [Peret; Siwan is lunar month 2 of Peret. Tammuz is] | lunar month 3 of Peret.’

— P.Omina ii 18-20

Genre: celestial divination
Provenance: Soknopaiou Nesos
Date: 2nd century CE; text from 7th cent. BCE

R. A. Parker, 1959, A Vienna Demotic Papyrus on Eclipse- and Lunar Omina , Providence . 9
