2 MUL.MEŠ ša₂ GIŠ.KUN mul.UR.A
2 kakkabu ša rapašti nēši

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 6/3/2024, 7:12:11 AM
Primary meaning δ+θ Leonis
Literal translation two stars of the rump of the lion
Variants
MUL₂ 2 ša₂ GIŠ.KUN-šu₂ kakkabu 2 ša rapaštišu in ziqpu star lists
2 MUL.MEŠ ša₂ GIŠ.KUN mul.UR.A 2 kakkabu ša rapašti nēši in GU text
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
δ+θ Leonis star: ziqpu star

MUL₂ 2 ša₂ GIŠ.KUN-šu₂ [...]

‘Two stars of his rump [...]’

— BM 36628+ obv. iii 11'

Genre: astral compendium
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 450-50 BCE

Steele, J. 2015, A Late Babylonian Compendium of Calendrical and Stellar Astrology, Journal of Cuneiform Studies 67, 187-215 196, 202

2 MUL.MEŠ ša₂ GIŠ.KUN mul.UR.A KIMIN KUN mul.UGA u ni-šik ša₂ mul.giš.GAN₂.UR₃ GU

‘The two stars of the rump of the lion or the tail of the raven and the bite of the harrow: a string.’

— BM 78161 (GU text) obv. 14-15

Genre: astronomical list
Provenance: Babylon or Sippar?
Date: ca. 7th-5th century BCE

Píngree, D. and C. Walker 1988, A Babylonian Star-Catalogue: BM 78161, Sachs Mem. Vol. 313-322 315-316
