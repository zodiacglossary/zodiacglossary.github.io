ἐπισεύω
episeuō

Greek verb

Last edited: 2/24/2025, 7:00:20 AM
Primary meaning to approach
Literal translation to rush at
Variants
ἐπισεύω episeuō
Edited by
Michael Zellmann-Rohrer
to approach planetary motion

Ζηνὸς ἐπεσσυμένου ἢ καὶ παρεόντος ἅμ’ αὐτῇ

‘when Zeus (Jupiter) has rushed at (approached) or is even present (in the same zodiac sign) with her (Venus)’

— Dorotheus 5.16.1–40

Genre: astrological treatise
Date: 1st cent. CE?
See also

〈excipio〉 ‘to be approached by, have another planet come into aspect’

〈adhaeresco〉 ‘to approach?’
