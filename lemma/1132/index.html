locus daemonis

Latin noun

Last edited: 12/12/2023, 12:01:02 PM
Primary meaning lot of the daemon
Literal translation place of the daemon
Variants
locus daemonis
daemonis locus
Edited by
Kierán Meinhardt
lot of the daemon astrological lot

Fortunae etiam locus et daemonis, geniturae dominus, quem Graeci oecodespoten vocant, diligenti ratione tractabitur, et eius etiam, qui actus decernit, efficacias explicabo;

‘The lot of fortune and of the daemon, the chart ruler, whom the Greeks call οἰκοδεσπότης will be treated diligently, and the effects of that [planet] which decides the occupations’

— Firmicus Maternus, Mathesis 4.0.4

Genre: astrological compendium
Provenance: Sicily
Date: ca. 334 CE

Firmicus Maternus. 1897. Ivlii Firmici Materni Matheseos Libri VIII, ed. W. Kroll, F. Skutsch. Stuttgart: B. G. Teubner. 1.196
