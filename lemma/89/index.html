mul.BABBAR
peṣû

Akkadian adjective

Last edited: 6/4/2024, 12:36:45 PM
Primary meaning Jupiter
Literal translation white, pale
Variants
mul.BABBAR peṣû
mul2.BABBAR peṣû
BABBAR peṣû
Edited by
Marvin Schreiber
Jupiter planet

MU.48 ŠE GE6 [23(?)] | lu2.TUR a-lid | U4.BI 20 ina 13.30 LU | 30 ina 10 GU | BABBAR ina SAG A | dil-bat KI 20 | GU4 KI 20 | GENNA ina ALLA | AN ina TIL ALLA

‘Year 48, month XII, night of the [23th(?)], | the child was born. | That day the sun was in 13;30 Aries, | the moon in 10° Aquarius, | Jupiter at the beginning of Leo, | Venus with the sun, | Mercury with the sun, | Saturn in Cancer, | Mars at the end of Cancer.’

— MLC 1870 (BH 5) 1-9

Genre: horoscope
Provenance: Uruk
Date: 3rd century BCE (date of birth in horoscope: S.E. 48 XII.[23] =-262 April 4)

Rochberg, F. 1998. Babylonian Horoscopes. Transactions of the American Philosophical Society, 88. 65

DU6 22 mul2.BABBAR ina GU UŠ | ina ŠE 2 ina KUN ŠU2

‘Month VII the 22nd, Jupiter's (2nd) stationary point in Aquarius. | Around the 2nd of month XII, (Jupiter's) last visibility in Pisces.’

— AO 17649 (BH 1) rev. 1-2

Genre: horoscope
Provenance: Nippur
Date: 5th-4th century BCE (date of birth in horoscope: Darius II., Year 13, X.24 = -409 Jan. 12/13)

Rochberg, F. 1998. Babylonian Horoscopes. Transactions of the American Philosophical Society, 88. 51

white, pale, bleached adjective
