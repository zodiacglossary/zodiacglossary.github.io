DILI ša₂ IGI ABSIN
ēdu ša maḫar šer'i

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 8/28/2025, 1:53:16 PM
Primary meaning γ Virginis
Literal translation single one in front of the furrow
Variants
e-du ša₂ IGI ABSIN ēdu ša maḫar šer'i
DILI ša₂ IGI ABSIN ēdu ša maḫar šer'i Mistakenly read as šur-ši ABSIN = "Root of Furrow" in Sachs JCS 4 (1952), and Roughton, Steele and Walker (2004), JCS: this star name is therefore a phantom.
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
γ Virginis star: normal star

Jones (2004) c19

GI₆ 11 sin TA DIRI ki E.A SIG DILI ša₂ IGI ABSIN 1 KUŠ₃ sin 1/2 KUŠ₃ ana NIM DIB

‘Night of (day) 11, when Sin emerged from a cloud, it was 1 cubit below the single (star) in front of the Furrow, passing 1/2 cubit to the east.’

— BM 45708 (ADRT III No. -125A) obv. 4

Genre: astronomical diary
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 126 BCE

Sachs, A. J., Hunger, H. 1996. Astronomical Diaries and Related Texts from Babylonia, Vol. III. Diaries from 164 B.C. to 61 B.C. Vienna, Austria: Verlag der Österreichischen Akademie der Wissenschaften. 258-259

šur-ši ABSIN 15

‘The root of the furrow: 15 (degrees of Virgo).’

— BM 46083

Genre: astral compendium
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 400-50 BCE

Roughton, N. A.; Steele, J. M.; and Walker, C. B. F. 2004 A Late Babylonian Normal and Ziqpu-Star Text. Archive for History of Exact Science 58: 537–572. 563-564
