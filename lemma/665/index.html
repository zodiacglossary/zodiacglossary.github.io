U₄.BI
inūšu

Akkadian adverb

Last edited: 6/24/2024, 7:10:07 AM
Primary meaning at that time
Literal translation that day
Variants
U₄.BI inūšu
i-nu-šu₂ inūšu
e-nu-šu inūšu
i-nu-ši inūšu
in₆-uš₂ inūšu
Edited by
Alessia Pilloni
at that time adverb of time

SIG 30 sin ar₂ ALLA IGI ka-bar 20 na SI GIN i-nu-šu₂ AN u GU₄.UD 4 KUŠ₃ ina IGI L[UGAL]

‘Month III, (the 1st of which was identical with) the 30th (of the preceding month), the moon became visible behind Cancer; it was thick; sunset to moonset: 20°; the north wind blew. At that time, Mars and Mercury were 4 cubits in front of α L[eonis].’

— VAT 4956 (ADRT I No. -567) 12

Genre: astronomical diary
Provenance: ?
Date: 568 BC

Sachs, A. J., Hunger, H. 1988. Astronomical Diaries and Related Texts from Babylonia, Vol. I: Diaries from 652 B.C. to 262 B.C. Vienna: Verlag der Österreichischen Akademie der Wissenschaften. 48-49

i-nu-šu₂ MUL₂.BABBAR ina MAŠ₂ dil-bat ina GIR₂.TAB

‘At that time, Jupiter was in Capricorn, Venus in Scorpio’

— BM 33667 u.e. 1

Genre: horoscope
Provenance: Babylon
Date: Seleucid era

Rochberg, F. 1998. Babylonian Horoscopes. Transactions of the American Philosophical Society, 88, 1–164. 73-74

that day adverb of time

U₄.BI sin ina A šamaš₂ ina 12,30 ina MAŠ.MAŠ

‘That day the moon was in Leo, the sun was in 12;30 Gemini’

— MLC 2190 obv. 3

Genre: horoscope
Provenance: Uruk
Date: Seleucid era

Rochberg, F. 1998. Babylonian Horoscopes. Transactions of the American Philosophical Society, 88, 1–164. 83-84
