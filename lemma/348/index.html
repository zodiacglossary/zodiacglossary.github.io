ME NIM.A
?

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 11/1/2023, 9:39:51 AM
Primary meaning time since sunrise
Literal translation day (since) rising (of sun)
Variants
u₄-me ša₂ NIM.A ūmu ša NIM.A
ME NIM.A ?
NIM ? rare abbreviation of ME NIM.A
ME NIM ? used in lunar system B for time of lunation for lunations between sunrise and noon
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
time since sunrise time interval

time between sunrise and a moment usually before noon

(21) 2 (22) A.RA₂ 2.30 5 DANNA u₄-me ša₂ NIM.A DU-ma

‘(21) 2 (22) you multiply by 2,30, (i.e.) 5 bēru, the time since sunrise (to New Moon), it is 5.’

— BM 32167+ (BMAPT No. 53) rev. ii 21-22

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-250 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer.

GAN 23 3 UŠ NIM

‘Month IX, (day) 23, 3 UŠ (time from) sunrise’

— BM 36731 obv. ii' 14'

Genre: mathematical astronomy: table of solstices, equinoxes, sirius phenomena
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 450-50 BCE

Neugebauer, O., Sachs, A. 1967, Atypical Astronomical Cuneiform Texts I, Journal of Cuneiform Studies 21, 183-218 183-190

28 50 ME NIM

‘(Day) 28, 50 (UŠ time after) sunrise’

— BM 34066+ (ACT No. 120) obv. 3 col. M (= time of New Moon)

Genre: mathematical astronomy: synodic table
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 2nd century BCE

Neugebauer, Otto, 1955, Astronomical Cuneiform Texts
