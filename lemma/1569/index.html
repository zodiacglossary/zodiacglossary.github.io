GIŠ.NIM
ṣītān

Akkadian adverb

Last edited: 2/11/2025, 1:20:51 PM
Primary meaning east
Literal translation at the rising (of the sun)
Variants
GIŠ.NIM ṣītān
Edited by
Marvin Schreiber
east cardinal direction

DIŠ ina iti.AB U4 15 KAM mul.SIM.MAḪ mul.ši-nu-nu-tu4 mul.IM.ŠEŠ | ina GIŠ.NIM IGI.LA2

‘¶ On the 15th day of Month X SIM.MAḪ, (i.e.) the Swallow (or) IM.ŠEŠ, | becomes visible in the east.’

— BM 86378 iii 7-8

Genre: schematic astronomy: mulapin
Provenance: ?
Date: ca. 626-539 BCE

Hunger, H. and Steele, J., 2019. The Babylonian Astronomical Compendium MUL.APIN. London and New York: Routledge. 51

ina 30 MU.MEŠ 7.20 KI-šu₂ ana GIŠ.NIM DIB

‘In 30 years its (Saturn's) position passes to the east by 7;20 (UŠ).’

— BM 41004 rev. 15

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon

Brack-Bernsen & Hunger 2005/6, AfO 51, 96-107 100
