lu-maš
lumāšu

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 9/23/2024, 7:38:19 AM
Primary meaning constellation
Literal translation constellation
Variants
lu-maš lumāšu
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
constellation astronomical term, celestial object
zodiac sign astronomical term, astrological concept, spatial concept

TA ŠU₂ ana IGI ki IGI-im-ma ina lu-maš.MEŠ DU₃-uš

‘From setting to (first) appearance you compute as before in (all) zodiac signs.’

— BM 34676 (BMAPT No. 13) rev. 32'

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-50 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer.

(6) KI sin u KI šamaš₂ ša₂ al-la 10 lu-maš DIRI GAM0 16 DU KI BAR.NUN ša₂ l[u-maš] (7) ša₂-šu₂ ki TAB TAB ki LA₂ LA₂

‘The position of Sin and the position of Šamaš: that which exceeds 10 (UŠ) of the zodiac sign you multiply times 0;16, add with the ṣiliptu-coefficient for the zodiac sign if it is increasing, subtract if it is decreasing.’

— BM 35399 (BMAPT No. 61A) obv. 6-7

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-50 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer.

in mathematical astronomy

(11') [... TA 10] ⸢ABSIN₀⸣ EN 10 GIR₂ lu-maš A.RA₂ ⸢20⸣ [x] LA₂ ša₂ u₄-me DU

‘(11') [... From 10] Vir until 10 Sco you multiply the zodiacal position by (0);20 [...] the length of daylight.’

Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-50 BCE
