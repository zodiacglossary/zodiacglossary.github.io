UŠ IGI-tu₂
nēmettu maḫrītu

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 6/26/2023, 4:39:56 PM
Primary meaning First Station
Literal translation first station
Variants
UŠ IGI-tu₂ nēmettu maḫrītu
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
First Station synodic phenomenon

for Mars, Jupiter, Saturn

(28') TA UŠ IGI-tu₂ EN UŠ ar₂-tu₂ (29') ša₂ ME 12 ana ar₂-ki-šu₂ LA₂-is-ma UŠ

‘(28') From the first station until the second station (29') per day (0);12 it recedes backwards, then it becomes stationary.’

— BM 34676 (BMAPT No. 13) rev. 28'-29'

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-50 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer.
