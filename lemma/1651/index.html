pedes posteriores et ungues sagittarii

Latin noun

Last edited: 6/3/2024, 7:48:48 AM
Primary meaning the hind-legs and hooves of the Archer
Literal translation hind-legs and hooves of the archer
Variants
pedes posteriores et ungues sagittarii
posteriores pedes et ungues sagittarii
Edited by
Kierán Meinhardt
the hind-legs and hooves of the Archer animal part

23°–25° Sagittarius

in posterioribus vero pedibus usque ad ungues tres quae sequuntur partes collocantur

‘in the hind-legs down to the hooves [of the Archer] the three following degrees are located’

— Firmicus Maternus, Mathesis 8.4.9

Genre: astrological compendium
Provenance: Sicily
Date: ca. 334 CE

Firmicus Maternus. 1897. Ivlii Firmici Materni Matheseos Libri VIII, ed. W. Kroll, F. Skutsch. Stuttgart: B. G. Teubner. 2.291
