spica

Latin noun

Last edited: 2/24/2025, 3:18:15 PM
Primary meaning the Ear of Corn
Literal translation ear of corn
Variants
spica
Edited by
Kierán Meinhardt
the Ear of Corn star

modern α Virginis

In Virginis signo parte X. oritur Spica.

‘At 10° of the sign of Virgo rises Spica.’

— Firmicus Maternus, Mathesis 8.11.3

Genre: astrological compendium
Provenance: Sicily
Date: ca. 334 CE

Firmicus Maternus. 1897. Ivlii Firmici Materni Matheseos Libri VIII, ed. W. Kroll, F. Skutsch. Stuttgart: B. G. Teubner. 2.305

In parte VIII. Virginis quicumque habuerint horoscopum, id est in ipsa Spica, erunt religiosi iusti

‘Whoever has the ascendant at 8° Virgo, i.e. exactly on Spica, will be religious and just’

— Firmicus Maternus, Mathesis 8.24.4

Genre: astrological compendium
Provenance: Sicily
Date: ca. 334 CE

Firmicus Maternus. 1897. Ivlii Firmici Materni Matheseos Libri VIII, ed. W. Kroll, F. Skutsch. Stuttgart: B. G. Teubner. 2.330
