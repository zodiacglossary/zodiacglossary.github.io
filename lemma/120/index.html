KI
qaqqaru

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 9/19/2023, 8:02:01 AM
Primary meaning position
Literal translation position
Variants
KI qaqqaru
qaq-qar qaqqaru
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
zodiacal longitude spatial concept

KI sin u KI šamaš₂ GAR ZI ša₂ sin is-suḫ TA KI s[in LA₂]

‘You put down the position of Sin and the position of Šamaš. The displacement of Sin you [subtract] from the position of Sin.’

— BM 55530 (BMAPT No. 61C) rev. 1

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-50 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer

(1) dil-b]at TA muḫ-ḫi UŠ ⸢ar₂⸣-tu₂ ina 2.⸢8⸣ [ME] (2) 2.8 ⸢KI⸣ DU

‘Dilbat (= Venus). From the second station in 2,8 [days] the position proceeds (by) 2,8.’

— BM 33552 (BMAPT No. 9) obv. 1-2

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 400-50 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer.

position spatial concept
region spatial concept

DU₃-šu₂ ša₂ NIM u SIG ša₂ sin AB₂ ana AB₂ 12 DAGAL ma-lak ᵈsin 2.24 MURUB₄-ti qaq-qar ki-ṣa-ri

‘’ Procedure for Sin’s ‘height and depth’, month by month. The width of the path of Sin is 12. The middle part, the region of the node, is 2,24.’

— BM 32167+ (BMAPT No. 53) obv. i 20'

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-250 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer.
