MUL₂ KUR ša₂ KIR₄ šil PA
kakkabu ba'lu ša appi šiltaḫ Pabilsag

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 10/27/2024, 4:14:59 PM
Primary meaning θ Ophiuchi
Literal translation the bright star of the tip of Pabilsag’s arrow
Variants
MUL₂ KUR ša₂ KIR₄ šil PA kakkabu ba'lu ša appi šiltaḫ Pabilsag
KIR₄ šil-taḫ PA 1/2 KUŠ₃ abbreviated name
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
θ Ophiuchi star: normal star

Jones (2004) c25

GE₆ 20 AN DUL ina ZALAG₂ sin ina IGI KIR₄ šil-taḫ PA 1/2 KUŠ₃

‘Night of (day) 20, rain DUL; last part of the night, Sin was 1/2 cubit in front of the tip of Pabilsag’s arrow.’

— BM 46009 (ADRT I No. -379) rev. 6'

Genre: astronomical diary
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 380 BCE

Sachs, A. J., Hunger, H. 1988. Astronomical Diaries and Related Texts from Babylonia, Vol. I: Diaries from 652 B.C. to 262 B.C. Vienna: Verlag der Österreichischen Akademie der Wissenschaften. 88-89

GE₆ 6 SAG GE₆ sin ar₂ MUL₂ KUR ša₂ KIR₄ šil-taḫ PA 1 2/3 KUŠ₃

‘Night of (day) 6, beginning of the night, Sin was 1 2/3 cubits behind the bright star of the tip of Pabilsag’s arrow.’
