GU4.UD
šiḫṭu

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 9/28/2023, 9:40:14 AM
Primary meaning Mercury
Literal translation strike, attack, raid
Variants

Edited by

Mercury planet

aš2-šu2 d.GU4.UD ina KI.MEŠ ša2 NITA u MUNUS KI 30 MUNUS U3.[TU]

‘Because Mercury in the regions of either a male or a female (stands) with the Moon: a female will be bor[n].’

— BM 35402 (LBAT 1593) obv. 8'

Genre: horoscopic astrology
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 4th-2nd century BCE

Reiner, E. 2000. Early Zodiologia and Related Matters. In: Wisdom, Gods and Literature, Studies in Assyriology in Honour of W. G. Lambert, eds. Andrew R. George and I. L. Finkel. Winona Lake, Indiana: Eisenbauns: 421-427. 422

d.30! u d.UTU d.Šul-pa-e3 mul.Dil-bat | mul.UDU.IDIM mul.SAG.UŠ d.UDU.IDIM.GU4.U[D] | mul4.Ṣal-bat-a-nu 7 mul.UDU.IDIM.ME[Š]

‘The Moon and the Sun, Jupiter, Venus, | the planet Saturn (and) Mercury, | Mars. Seven planets.’

— K 2067 (CT 26 45) 19-21

Genre: star list
Provenance: Nineveh
Date: 7th century BCE

Koch-Westenholz, U., 1995. Mesopotamian Astrology. An Introduction to Babylonian and Assyrian Celestial Divination. Copenhagen: Museum Tusculanum Press. 200

ina E2 ni-ṣir-tu4 ša2 GU4.UD a-lid

‘He was born in the house of secret of Mercury.’

— BM 47721 (= BH 6) obv. 4'

Genre: horoscope
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 3rd century BCE

Rochberg, F. 1998. Babylonian Horoscopes. Transactions of the American Philosophical Society, 88, 1–164. 69

MU.48 ŠE GE6 [23(?)] | lu2.TUR a-lid | U4.BI 20 ina 13.30 LU | 30 ina 10 GU | BABBAR ina SAG A | dil-bat KI 20 | GU4 KI 20 | GENNA ina ALLA | AN ina TIL ALLA

‘Year 48, month XII, night of the [23th(?)], | the child was born. | That day the sun was in 13;30 Aries, | the moon in 10° Aquarius, | Jupiter at the beginning of Leo, | Venus with the sun, | Mercury with the sun, | Saturn in Cancer, | Mars at the end of Cancer.’

— MLC 1870 (BH 5) 1-9

Genre: horoscope
Provenance: Uruk
Date: 3rd century BCE (date of birth in horoscope: S.E. 48 XII.[23] =-262 April 4)

Rochberg, F. 1998. Babylonian Horoscopes. Transactions of the American Philosophical Society, 88. 65

strike, attack, raid
