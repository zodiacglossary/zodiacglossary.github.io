addo

Latin verb

Last edited: 11/25/2024, 9:04:13 AM
Primary meaning to add
Literal translation to put
Variants
addo
Edited by
Kierán Meinhardt
to add mathematical operation, arithmetical operation

quod fuerit laevum praelataque signa sequetur tricenos annos duplicat, tris insuper addit.

‘The trigon on the left and following in the wake of the preceding signs doubles thirty years and adds three over and above.’

— Manilius, Astronomica 3.601

Genre: astrological treatise

Manilius. Astronomica. Edited and translated by G. P. Goold. Loeb Classical Library 469. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press, 1977. 212-213

to assign astrological concept

of astrological influence

Quattuor appositis Centaurus partibus effert sidera et ex ipso mores nascentibus addit.

‘After a further four degrees the Centaur rears his stars and from his own nature assigns qualities to his progeny.’

— Manilius, Astronomica 5.348 f

Genre: astrological treatise

Manilius. Astronomica. Edited and translated by G. P. Goold. Loeb Classical Library 469. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press, 1977. 328-329
