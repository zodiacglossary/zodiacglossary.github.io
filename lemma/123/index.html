SIG
šuplu

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 11/9/2023, 2:26:30 PM
Primary meaning distance below ecliptic
Literal translation depth
Variants
SIG šuplu
šup-lu šuplu
BUR₃ šuplu
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
distance below ecliptic astronomical quantity

NIM u SIG ša₂ sin GAR

‘You put down the height and depth of Sin.’

— BM 55530 (BMAPT No. 61C) rev. 5

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-50 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer

šu-qa u šup-lu ša₂ ᵈsin KIN.KIN-ma

‘The 'height' and 'depth' of Sin you investigate, and (...)’

— BM 35325 (LBAT 1600) rev. 9'

Genre: astrology
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 450-50 BCE

Sachs, Abraham, Strassmaier, Johann N., Pinches, Theophilus. G. (1955). Late Babylonian astronomical and related texts.

NIM u₃ BUR₃ ša₂ sin ana DU₃-ka

‘In order for you to compute the "height" and "depth" of Sin’

— BM 41004

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon

Brack-Bernsen & Hunger 2005/6, AfO 51, 96-107

minimum mathematical quantity

(3) ša₂ GENNA 25.24.5 NIM ⸢2⸣[2.41.25 SIG] (4) 12 taš an-na-a [ša₂ ME.MEŠ]

‘(3) For Saturn: the ‘height’ is 25;24,5, [the ‘depth’ is 22;41,25], (4) the difference is (0);12; thus [for the ‘days’].’

— A 3418 (BMAPT No. 41) 3-4

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Uruk
Date: ca. 170-200 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer
