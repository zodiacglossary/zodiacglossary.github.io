ana tar-ṣi
ana tarṣi

Akkadian preposition

Last edited: 5/6/2024, 7:31:31 AM
Primary meaning opposite
Literal translation opposite
Variants
ana tar-ṣi ana tarṣi
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
opposite mathematical expression

used for associating one number with another number

(14) ana tar-ṣi 1.57.58.8.53.20 TAB-u₂ 4.53.14.4.26.40 GAR-an (15) ša₂ al-la 1.57.58.8.53.20 TAB-u₂ DIRI EN 1.58.15.5⸢5⸣.33.20 TAB-u₂ (16) A.RA₂ 5.20 DU KI 4.53.14.4.26.40 TAB-ma GAR-an

‘(14) Opposite 1,57;58,8,53,20, increasing, you put 4,53;14,4,26,40. (15) Whatever (the amount) by which it exceeds 1,57;58,8,53,20, increasing, until 1,58;15,55,33,20, increasing, (16) you multiply by 5;20, add with 4,53;14,4,26,40, and put down.’

— BM 32167+ (BMAPT No. 53) rev. i 14-16

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-250 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer. 362-363
