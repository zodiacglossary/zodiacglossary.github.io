al
alla

Akkadian preposition

Last edited: 6/24/2024, 7:39:35 AM
Primary meaning beyond
Literal translation beyond
Variants
al alla Abbr. in Late Babylonian astro. text.
al-la alla
Edited by
Marvin Schreiber
beyond, over and above, (more, or less) than preposition

iti.KIN 13 KI ŠU2 20 | AN.GE6 30 al šal ḪAB-rat | DIRI GAR-an ad2 E3-a | ina ZIB.ME

‘Month VI, the 13th, with the setting of the sun | a lunar eclipse, exceeding 1/3 of the disk, | occured; (the moon) was darkly when it rose | in Pisces.’

— BM 38104 (BH 27) 5-8

Genre: horoscope
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 1st century BCE (date of birth in horoscope: 243 S.E. I.20 = -68 Apr.16)

Rochberg, F. 1998. Babylonian Horoscopes. Transactions of the American Philosophical Society, 88. 137

[iti.NE 14.KAM AN.GE6 30] | ina ZIB.ME al-la 2-ta [ŠU.2.MEŠ] | ḪAB-rat DIR GAR-an

‘[Month V, on the 14th, a lunar eclipse] | in Pisces, beyond two-[thirds] | of the disk it (the moon) made.’

— BM 33018 (BH 21) rev. 1-3

Genre: horoscope
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 2nd century BCE (date of birth in horoscope: S.E. 187 VI.22 = -124 Oct. 1)

Rochberg, F. 1998. Babylonian Horoscopes. Transactions of the American Philosophical Society, 88. 117
