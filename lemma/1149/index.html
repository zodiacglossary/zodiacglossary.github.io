locus necessarius

Latin noun

Last edited: 1/12/2024, 3:50:21 PM
Primary meaning important place
Literal translation necessary place
Variants
locus necessarius
necessarius locus
Edited by
Kierán Meinhardt
important place astrological concept

presumably the cardines and places 11, 5, 9 and 3 (secunda)

sed et ipsos facit aut vitiosos debiles aut oligochronios aut malo quodam exitu intereuntes, praesertim si in ista coniunctione in necessariis geniturae locis fuerit inventa, idest si [in] horoscopo partili radiatione iungatur.

‘but it makes them also weak and sickly or short-lived or die some kind of evil death, especially if [the Moon] is found in this aspect in important places [of the chart], i.e. if she aspects the degree of the ascendant’

— Firmicus Maternus, Mathesis 4.2.2

Genre: astrological compendium
Provenance: Sicily
Date: ca. 334 CE

Firmicus Maternus. 1897. Ivlii Firmici Materni Matheseos Libri VIII, ed. W. Kroll, F. Skutsch. Stuttgart: B. G. Teubner. 1.200
