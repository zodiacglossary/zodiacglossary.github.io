δυσμή
dusmē

Greek noun

Last edited: 12/2/2024, 3:55:50 PM
Primary meaning setting
Literal translation setting
Variants
δυσμή dusmē
δυθμή duthmē e.g. in Callimachus
Edited by
Thomas Peeters
setting astronomical event

Ἐμοὶ γάρ, ὦ ἄνδρες, ὅπερ καὶ πρότερον εἶπον, φίλος ὢν Σώστρατος καὶ οἰκείως διακείμενος ἀπαντήσας ἐξ ἀγροῦ περὶ ἡλίου δυσμὰς συνεδείπνει, καὶ ἐπειδὴ καλῶς εἶχεν αὐτῷ, ἀπιὼν ᾤχετο.

‘For, gentleman, as I have said before as well, Sostratos, being a friend of mine and disposed as a house friend, came from the country to meet me around sunset and had dinner with me, and after it had been going well for him, he departed and left.’

— Lysias 1, On the Murder of Eratosthenes 39

Genre: oratory
Date: 5th-4th century BCE

Lysias. 1930. Lysias with an English translation by W.R.M. Lamb, ed. W.R.M. Lamb. Cambridge Massachusetts: Harvard University Press & London: William Heinemann.

west cardinal direction

often as compound ἡλίου δυσμή (setting of the sun)

Ὡς δὲ ἀπὸ τοῦ Στρυμόνος ἐπορεύετο ὁ στρατός, ἐνθαῦτα πρὸς ἡλίου δυσμέων ἐστὶ αἰγιαλὸς ἐν τῷ οἰκημένην Ἄργιλον πόλιν Ἑλλάδα παρεξήιε· αὕτη δὲ καὶ ἡ κατύπερθε ταύτης καλέεται Βισαλτίη.

‘And as the army marched from the Strumon, it passed Argilos, there in the west is a sea-shore on which it is situated, a Greek city: and this itself and its upper country are called Bisaltiē.’

— Herodotus 7.115.1

Genre: historiography
Date: 5th century BCE

Herodotus. 1920. Herodotus, with an English translation by A.D. Godley, ed. A.D. Godley. Cambridge Massachusetts: Harvard University Press.
