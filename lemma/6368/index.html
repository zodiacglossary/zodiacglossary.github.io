दिनकृत्
dinakṛt

Sanskrit noun
loaned from Sanskrit

Last edited: 10/26/2025, 6:07:32 PM
Primary meaning Sun
Literal translation Sun
Variants
दिनकर dinakara Sun
Edited by
Poorvaa Malhotra
Sun celestial object

पूर्वार्धमोजेषु गृहेषु सौर्यं तेष्वेव पश्चार्धमुशन्ति चान्द्रम् । युग्मेषु पूर्वार्धविभुः शशाङ्कः पश्चार्धनाथो दिनकृत् समेषु ॥३१॥

pūrvārdhamojeṣu gṛheṣu sauryaṃ teṣveva paścārdhamuśanti cāndram | yugmeṣu pūrvārdhavibhuḥ śaśāṅkaḥ paścārdhanātho dinakṛt sameṣu ||31||

‘In the odd signs the Sun governs the first half, and the Moon governs the second half of each house (sign). Among the even signs the Moon rules the first half, and the Sun rules the latter half’

— Yavanajātaka 1.31

Genre: astrological treatise
Provenance: India
Date: 4th-6th century CE

Pingree, David. The Yavanajātaka of Sphujidhvaja. Harvard Oriental Series, vol. 48. Cambridge: Harvard University Press 49
