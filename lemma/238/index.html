ina ŠU₂ IGI
ina rabê nanmuru

Akkadian verb

Last edited: 6/26/2023, 6:37:16 PM
Primary meaning Evening First
Literal translation to appear in the West
Variants
ina ŠU₂ IGI ina rabê nanmuru
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
Evening First synodic phenomenon

(1) ⸢u₄⸣-mu dil-bat ina ŠU₂ IGI lu ina KUR ŠU₂-u₂ U₄.MEŠ u KI.MEŠ ana igi-gub-u₂ [x] (2) ina ŠU.2-ka tu-kal

‘(1) The day when Venus appears in the West or sets in the East: (2) you hold the "days" and the positions for the igigubbû-coefficients [...] in your hands.’

— BM 34221 (BMAPT No. 46) rev. ii 1-2

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-50 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer.

10 GU4.UD ina ŠU₂ ina UR.A 3 KUŠ3 ina IGI dil-bat ana ŠU₂ IGI 16 [NA-su in 8 IGI]

‘(Day) 10, Mercury appears in the West in the Lion, 3 cubits in front of Venus to the West, its [NA (sunset to setting of Mercury)] is 16 (UŠ), the (ideal first) appearance was on (day) 8.]’

— BM 32149+ (ADRT I No. -366A) obv. 38'

Genre: astronomical diary
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 367 BCE

Sachs, A. J., Hunger, H. 1988. Astronomical Diaries and Related Texts from Babylonia, Vol. I: Diaries from 652 B.C. to 262 B.C. Vienna: Verlag der Österreichischen Akademie der Wissenschaften. 136-137
