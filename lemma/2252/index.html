Αἰδώς
Aidōs

Greek noun
loaned from Akkadian

Last edited: 7/15/2024, 7:16:02 AM
Primary meaning Moon
Literal translation Moon
Variants
Αἰδώς Aidōs restored reading
Ἀϊδὴς Aidēs rare
Αἰδώ Aidō rare
Αἰδῶ Aidō rare
Edited by
Thomas Peeters
Moon luminary, planet, deity

αἰδώς· *αἰσχύνη ... τὴν τοῦ σώματος αἰσχρότητα, τὰ αἰδοῖα. καὶ ἡ σελήνη, παρὰ Χαλδαίοις

‘Aidōs: *shame ... the ugliness of the body, the private parts. And the Moon, according to the Chaldeans.’

— Hesychius α 1799

Genre: lexicon
Provenance: Alexandria
Date: 4th-6th century CE

Hesychius Alexandrinus. 2018. Hesychii Alexandrini Lexicon, Vol. 1 (A - Delta), ed. I.C. Cunningham. Berlin, Boston: Walter de Gruyter. 85

In the manuscripts, the readings ἀϊδὴς, αἰδώ and αἰδῶ are attested.
