मघा
Maghā

Sanskrit noun
loaned from Sanskrit

Primary meaning Regulus (α Leonis)
Literal translation Mighty
Variants
मघा Maghā Mighty
Edited by
Poorvaa Malhotra
Maghā star, part of constellation

Called as the Mighty Star, Magha, is the tenth Nakshatra or star in the constellation of Leo (0° to 13°20'). It corresponds to star Regulus or Alpha-Leonis

तस्यार्धमार्कं विहितं मघादि सर्पादि शाशाङ्कमतोऽपरार्धम्। क्रमेण सूर्यः प्रददौ ग्रहेभ्यः क्षेत्राणि चान्द्राणि तथोत्क्रमेण ॥२९॥

tasyārdhamārkaṃ vihitaṃ maghādi sarpādi śāśāṅkamato'parārdham| krameṇa sūryaḥ pradadau grahebhyaḥ kṣetrāṇi cāndrāṇi tathotkrameṇa ||29||

‘The ‘solar half’ of the zodiac begins with the Maghā Nakṣatra (at 0° Leo). The ‘lunar half’ begins with the Sarpa Nakṣatra (at 29° 29′ 29″ Cancer). The Sun is assigned to the clockwise direction, and the signs going in the counter clockwise direction are those of the Moon (the lunar zodiac)’

— Yavanajātaka 1.29

Genre: astrological treatise
Provenance: India
Date: 4th-6th century CE

Pingree, David. The Yavanajātaka of Sphujidhvaja. Harvard Oriental Series, vol. 48. Cambridge: Harvard University Press 48
