cornua cervi

Latin noun

Last edited: 12/12/2023, 11:38:06 AM
Primary meaning the horns of the Stag
Literal translation horns of the stag
Variants
cornua cervi
cervi cornua
Edited by
Kierán Meinhardt
the horns of the Stag animal part

Quicumque itaque horoscopum in cornibus Cervi habuerit, si Mars in ipsa parte fuerit inventus, erit praepositus armorum

‘Therefore, whoever has the ascendant in the horns of the Stag, if Mars is located on this very degree, will be an army commander’

— Firmicus Maternus, Mathesis 8.30.12

Genre: astrological compendium
Provenance: Sicily
Date: ca. 334 CE

Firmicus Maternus. 1897. Ivlii Firmici Materni Matheseos Libri VIII, ed. W. Kroll, F. Skutsch. Stuttgart: B. G. Teubner. 2.356

See also

〈cornua capricorni〉 ‘the horns of Capricorn’
