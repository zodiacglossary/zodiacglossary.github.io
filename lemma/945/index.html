plenus

Latin adjective

Last edited: 12/12/2023, 12:32:29 PM
Primary meaning full
Literal translation full
Variants
plenus
Edited by
Kierán Meinhardt
full lunar phase

si vero plena lumine uel crescens Luna Veneri vel Marti se aliqua radiatione coniunxerit, maximas calamitates et maxima facit infortunia concitari.

‘But if a full or waxing Moon comes into aspect with Venus or Mars, it brings about the greatest catastrophes and stirs up enormous ill fortune’

— Firmicus Maternus, Mathesis 2.7.3

Genre: astrological compendium
Provenance: Sicily
Date: ca. 334 CE

Firmicus Maternus. 1897. Ivlii Firmici Materni Matheseos Libri VIII, ed. W. Kroll, F. Skutsch. Stuttgart: B. G. Teubner. 1.49

full astrological concept

a degree which belongs to a decan

Plenae sunt itaque is partes, in quibus decani fuerint inventi, vacuae vero, ad quas partes numquam decanorum numen accedat.

‘Therefore, full are the degrees in which the decans are found, empty however are those to which no decan belongs’

— Firmicus Maternus, Mathesis 4.22.3

Genre: astrological compendium
Provenance: Sicily
Date: ca. 334 CE

Firmicus Maternus. 1897. Ivlii Firmici Materni Matheseos Libri VIII, ed. W. Kroll, F. Skutsch. Stuttgart: B. G. Teubner. 1.265
