aquilo

Latin noun

Last edited: 2/13/2024, 12:50:53 PM
Primary meaning north wind
Literal translation north wind
Variants
aquilo
Edited by
Kierán Meinhardt
north wind type of wind

[...] quae sit aquiloni pars terrae [...] subiecta

‘which part of the earth is subject to the north wind’

— Firmicus Maternus, Mathesis 1.0.5

Genre: astrological compendium
Provenance: Sicily
Date: ca. 334 CE

Firmicus Maternus. 1897. Ivlii Firmici Materni Matheseos Libri VIII, ed. W. Kroll, F. Skutsch. Stuttgart: B. G. Teubner. 1.3

north cardinal direction
north-north-east wind type of wind

A septemtrionali latere summus ere summus est aquilo, medius septemtrio, imus θρασκίας; huic deest apud nos vocabulum.

‘In the northern section the highest is Aquilo [N.N.E.], the middle Septemtrio [N.], the lowest is Thrascias [N.N.W.], for which there is lacking a word in our language.’

— Seneca, Natural Questions

Genre: philosophy
Date: 62/63 CE

Seneca. Natural Questions, Volume II: Books 4-7. Translated by Thomas H. Corcoran. Loeb Classical Library 457. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press, 1972. 108-109
