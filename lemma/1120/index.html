χρόνος
chronos

Greek noun

Last edited: 12/11/2024, 4:05:20 PM
Primary meaning time
Literal translation time
Variants
χρόνος chronos
Edited by
Thomas Peeters
time astronomical quantity

(...) ἔστι γὰρ ἐνιαύσιος χρόνος, ἐν ᾧ ὁ ἥλιος περιπορεύεται τὸν ζῳδιακὸν κύκλον καὶ ἀπὸ τοῦ αὐτοῦ σημείου ἐπὶ τὸ αὐτὸ σημεῖον ἀποκαθίσταται.

‘(...) for it is the time of a year, in which the sun goes around the zodiac and is returned from the same point to the same point.’

— Geminus 1.7

Genre: astronomy
Date: 1st century BCE

Geminus. 1975. Géminos, Introduction aux Phénomènes, ed. G. Aujac. Paris: Les Belles Lettres. 2

time period time interval

Αἱ πᾶσαι οὖν ἡμέραι τούτων τῶν δʹ χρόνων συντιθέμεναι ποιοῦσι τξεʹ δʹʹ, ὅσαιπερ ἦσαν αἱ τοῦ ἐνιαυτοῦ.

‘All the days of these 4 time periods then, when put together, make 365 and a 4th, as much as those of the year were.’

— Geminus 1.17

Genre: astronomy
Date: 1st century BCE

Geminus. 1975. Géminos, Introduction aux Phénomènes, ed. G. Aujac. Paris: Les Belles Lettres. 5
