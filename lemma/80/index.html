UŠ
šuššān

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 11/3/2024, 3:13:00 PM
Primary meaning degree
Literal translation (time) degree
Variants
UŠ šuššān
šu-uš-ša2-an šuššān Rare phonetic writing (see Ossendrijver 2022 NABU No. 68)
šu-ši šūši Rare phonetic writing (see Ossendrijver 2022 NABU No. 68)
Edited by
Alessia Pilloni
(time) degree unit of time

[... ki?] ⸢iz⸣-ku-u₂ MUL₂.BABBAR E-a ina 10 UŠ ME ana ŠU₂ šamaš₂

kizkū MUL₂.BABBAR E-a ina 10 UŠ ME ana ŠU₂ šamaš₂

‘[... when?] it had cleared, Jupiter came out; (the eclipse began) at 10° before sunset’

— BM 40122 (LBAT 0218) o.8'.

Genre: astronomical diary
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 367 BCE

Sachs, Abraham, Strassmaier, Johann N., Pinches, Theophilus. G. (1955). Late Babylonian astronomical and related texts. Brown University studies. J. Koch, AfO 38/39 103-107; Sachs, Abraham J., Hunger, Hermann. Astronomical Diaries 1, no. -304; Reiner, Fs Borger (CM 10) p 298.

UŠ unit of celestial position
UŠ unit of length

30 šu-uš-ša2-an be2-e-ri

‘30 šuššān is a bēru.’

— W 23281 obv. i 27

Genre: mathematical compendium
Provenance: Uruk
Date: ca. 445-385 BCE

Ossendrijver 2022, On the unit UŠ = šuššān, NABU 2022/2, No. 68, 156–157
