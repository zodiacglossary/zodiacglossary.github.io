mixtura

Latin noun

Last edited: 9/2/2024, 10:38:32 AM
Primary meaning configuration of planets
Literal translation mixture
Variants
mixtura
Edited by
Kierán Meinhardt
configuration of planets astrological concept

Sed antequam ad explicationem mixturarum sermo noster accedat, pauca tibi de splendidarum stellarum dicam

‘But before we come to speak about configurations of the planets, I want to tell you something about the bright stars’

— Firmicus Maternus, Mathesis 6.1.10

Genre: astrological compendium
Provenance: Sicily
Date: ca. 334 CE

Firmicus Maternus. 1897. Ivlii Firmici Materni Matheseos Libri VIII, ed. W. Kroll, F. Skutsch. Stuttgart: B. G. Teubner. 2.69

synthesis of astrological interpretations practice

sed mihi templorum tantum nunc iura canentur; mox veniet mixtura suis cum viribus omnis, cum bene materies steterit percognita rerum non interpositis turbatarum undique membris.

‘But I now shall sing only of the ordinances of the temples; later the whole complex design will appear with its full force, when the constituent parts of the universe are firmly grasped and not made confusing with portions scattered everywhere.’

— Manilius, Astronomica 3.586 ff

Genre: astrological treatise

Manilius. Astronomica. Edited and translated by G. P. Goold. Loeb Classical Library 469. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press, 1977. 210-211
