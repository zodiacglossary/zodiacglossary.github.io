IR₂
bikītu

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 11/8/2024, 3:01:59 PM
Primary meaning phase of maximal eclipse
Literal translation weeping
Variants
IR₂ bikītu
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
phase of maximal eclipse astronomical term, lunar phenomenon, solar phenomenon, eclipse phenomenon

said of lunar and solar eclipses

(13') 29 24 ME NIM-a šamaš₂ AN.MI A₂ ⸢ULU₃⸣ [u] ⸢MAR⸣ ki-i TAB-[u₂ ...] (14') [dil]-bat GU₄.UD u MUL₂ ŠID.MEŠ IGI.MEŠ MUL₂.BABBAR u AN ša₂ ina bi-ib-lu ina AN.MI-šu₂ IGI.MEŠ [...] (15') TA MAR u ULU₃ ana SI u KUR iš-ta-ḫaṭ 35 GAR IR₂ u ZALAG₂-ru

‘(13') (Day) 29, (at) 24 (UŠ) after sunrise, solar eclipse; when it began on the south and west side, [...] (14') [Ven]us, Mercury, and the counting stars were visible; Jupiter and Mars, which were in their period of invisibility, were visible in its eclipse [...] (15') it threw off (the shadow) from west and south to north and east; 35 (UŠ) onset, "weeping", and clearing.’

— BM 45745 (ADRT III No. -136B) rev. 13'-15'

Genre: astronomical diary
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 137 BCE

Sachs, A. J., Hunger, H. 1996. Astronomical Diaries and Related Texts from Babylonia, Vol. III. Diaries from 164 B.C. to 61 B.C. Vienna, Austria: Verlag der Österreichischen Akademie der Wissenschaften. 182-183
