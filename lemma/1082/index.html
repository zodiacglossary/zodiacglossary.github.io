GAR
šakānu

Akkadian verb

Last edited: 7/15/2024, 7:42:18 AM
Literal translation to place
Variants
GAR šakānu
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
to put down mathematical operation

said of mathematical results

ana tar-ṣi 2.13.20 LA₂-u₂ 2.40 GAR-an

‘Opposite 2,13;20, decreasing, you put 2,40.’

— BM 32167+ (BMAPT No. 53) rev. i 3

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-250 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer. 362-363

to cause divine agency

said of eclipses

mul₂.UD.KA.DUḪ.A a-dir sin ina EN.NUN.USAN AN.MI GAR-ma

‘The-Demon-with-the-gaping-mouth-star is dark: (it means that) Sin causes an eclipse in the evening watch.’

— AO 6455 (TU 11) obv. 1

Genre: astrology and goal-year astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Uruk
Date: ca. 200 BCE

Brack-Bernsen, Lis, and Hunger, Hermann. 2002. TU 11. A Collection of Rules for the Prediction of Lunar Phases and of Month Lengths. SCIAMVS 3: 3–90.
