ina NIM IGI
ina ... nanmuru

Akkadian verb

Last edited: 1/26/2024, 12:31:50 PM
Primary meaning Morning First
Literal translation to appear in the East
Variants
ina NIM IGI ina x nanmuru Akkadian reading of NIM uncertain (elê?)
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
Morning First synodic phenomenon

in astronomical diaries and related texts

3 dil-bat ina NIM ar2 GIGIR IGI

‘(Month III), (day) 3, Venus appears in the East behind the Chariot.’

— VAT 4924 (ADRT I No. -418) obv. 11

Genre: astronomical diary
Provenance: Babylon or Uruk
Date: 420 BCE

Sachs, A. J., Hunger, H. 1988. Astronomical Diaries and Related Texts from Babylonia, Vol. I: Diaries from 652 B.C. to 262 B.C. Vienna: Verlag der Österreichischen Akademie der Wissenschaften.

22 GU4.UD ina NIM ina PA.BIL IGI

‘(Month VIII), (day) 22, Mercury appears in the East in Pabilsag.’

— BM 35195 (ADRT I No. -378) obv? 9'

Genre: astronomical diary
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 379 BCE

Sachs, A. J., Hunger, H. 1988. Astronomical Diaries and Related Texts from Babylonia, Vol. I: Diaries from 652 B.C. to 262 B.C. Vienna: Verlag der Österreichischen Akademie der Wissenschaften. 92
