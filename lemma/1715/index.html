aversus

Latin adjective

Last edited: 11/11/2024, 9:16:13 AM
Primary meaning unaspected
Literal translation averse
Variants
aversus
Edited by
Kierán Meinhardt
unaspected astrological concept

quaedam vero aversa esse ab horoscopo et quodam modo aliena, quae non inmerito pigra diximus

‘these we call averse [unaspected] to the ascendant and in some way alien, which we rightly call dull’

— Firmicus Maternus, Mathesis 2.23.1

Genre: astrological compendium
Provenance: Sicily
Date: ca. 334 CE

Firmicus Maternus. 1897. Ivlii Firmici Materni Matheseos Libri VIII, ed. W. Kroll, F. Skutsch. Stuttgart: B. G. Teubner. 1.70

rising hind-first spatial concept, zodiac sign quality

of signs, i.e. Taurus, Gemini, Cancer

ne mirere moras, cum sol aversa per astra aestivum tardis attollat mensibus annum.

‘so wonder not at the delay when in tardy months the Sun carries summertide aloft through signs which rise hind-first.’

— Manilius, Astronomica 2.201 f

Genre: astrological treatise

Manilius. Astronomica. Edited and translated by G. P. Goold. Loeb Classical Library 469. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press, 1977. 98-99
