Mecheir (month VI) month, time interval

month VI in the Alexandrian and Egyptian calendar

Μετὰ δὲ ἔτη τμα Αἰγυπτιακὰ καὶ ἡμέρας πζ ἡμεῖς ἐτηρήσαμεν τι η τῆς Τραιανοῦ καίσαρος ἡγεμονίας πρὸ μιᾶς καλανδῶν Ἰανουαρίων Μεχεὶρ ζ εἰς τὴν η.

‘And after 341 Egyptian years and 87 days we observed something, the 8th (year) of emperor Trajan's rule, the day before the first - the kalends - of January, Mecheir 7 until the 8th.’

— P. Oxy. LXI 4133 3-8

Genre: astronomical treatise
Provenance: Oxyrhynchus
Date: 2nd century CE

Jones, A. (ed.), 1999. Astronomical papyri from Oxyrhynchus (P. Oxy. 4133-4300a), Volumes I and II. Philadelphia: American Philosophical Society. I: 69-80 + II: 2-5

This papyrus contains parts of a treatise on planetary theory which might be attributed to Menelaus of Alexandria. 341 Egyptian years is most likely a scribal error for 344 years.
