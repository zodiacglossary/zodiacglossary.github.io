GI₆
mūšu

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 10/10/2023, 1:03:16 PM
Primary meaning night
Literal translation night
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
night time interval

(14) NA ana DU₃-ka ki si-man ⸢ša₂ GI₆ DU⸣ al-la LA₂ ša₂ GI₆ ⸢DIRI⸣ LA₂ ša₂ GI₆ ⸢TA⸣ GI₆ DU (15) NIM

‘(14) In order for you to construct NA. If the time by which the night has progressed exceeds the length of night, you deduct the length of night from (the time by which) the night has progressed.’

— BM 35399 (BMAPT No. 61A) obv. 14-15

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-50 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer. 398-399

GI₆ 8 SAG GI₆ sin ina IGI LUGAL 2 KUŠ₃

‘Night of (day) 8, beginning of the night, Sin was 2 cubits in front of the King (star).’

— BM 41616+ (ADRT II No. -255) obv. 4'

Genre: astronomical diary
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 256 BCE

Sachs, A. J., Hunger, H. 1989. Astronomical Diaries and Related Texts from Babylonia, Vol. II: Diaries from 261 B.C. to 165 B.C. Vienna: Verlag der Österreichischen Akademie der Wissenschaften. 16-17
