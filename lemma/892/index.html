regio

Latin noun

Last edited: 9/2/2024, 8:21:34 AM
Primary meaning region
Literal translation region
Variants
regio
Edited by
Kierán Meinhardt
region (of the sky)

At illa regio caelestis per triginta annos velocissimo sideri viam praestat nusquam resistenti sed aequaliter cito.

‘But in the heavenly region the swiftest star, which never stops and maintains a constant velocity, has a journey of thirty years.’

— Seneca, Natural Questions 1.0.13

Genre: philosophy
Date: 62/63 CE

Seneca. Natural Questions, Volume I: Books 1-3. Translated by Thomas H. Corcoran. Loeb Classical Library 450. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press, 1971. 10-11

sphere astronomical term

tu vero, Gradive Mars rutilo semper horrore metuende, qui in tertiis caeli regionibus contineris

‘and you, Mars Gradivus, always with your fearful red color, whose home is the third region of the sky’

— Firmicus Maternus, Mathesis 1.10.14

Genre: astrological compendium
Provenance: Sicily
Date: ca. 334 CE

Firmicus Maternus. 1897. Ivlii Firmici Materni Matheseos Libri VIII, ed. W. Kroll, F. Skutsch. Stuttgart: B. G. Teubner. 1.38

region (on earth)

in nulla fallet regione horoscopos umquam

‘in no latitude will the Horoscope ever elude you’

— Manilius, Astronomica 3.440

Genre: astrological treatise

Manilius. Astronomica. Edited and translated by G. P. Goold. Loeb Classical Library 469. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press, 1977. 196-197
