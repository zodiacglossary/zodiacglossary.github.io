ὥρα
hōra

Greek noun

Last edited: 12/3/2024, 3:38:25 PM
Primary meaning hour
Literal translation hour
Variants
ὥρα hōra
ὅρα hora rare
ὥρ hōr abbreviation, often written as a ligature with the ρ written through the middle of the ω
Edited by
Thomas Peeters
hour time interval, unit of time

ὡρῶν ιεʹ κλίμα εʹ

‘15 hours clime 5’

— P. Oxy. LXI 4167 fr. 4, vi-vii, 1-2

Genre: mathematical astronomy: epoch table
Provenance: Oxyrhynchus
Date: 3rd-4th century CE

Jones, A. (ed.), 1999. Astronomical papyri from Oxyrhynchus (P. Oxy. 4133-4300a), Volumes I and II. Philadelphia: American Philosophical Society. I: 160-162 + II: 118-129

This papyrus contains parts of Ptolemy's Handy Tables.

Γένεσις Ἀπίας. (ἔτους) αʹ Κάρου καὶ Καρίνου καὶ Νουμεριανοῦ, Παοῖνι κεʹ εἰς κϛʹ, νοικτὸς ὅρᾳ δʹ.

‘Nativity of Apia. Year 1 of Karos and Karinos and Noumerianos, Pauni 25 till 26, hour 4 of night.’

— P. Oxy. LXI 4258

Genre: horoscope
Provenance: Oxyrhynchus
Date: after 283 CE

Jones, A. (ed.), 1999. Astronomical papyri from Oxyrhynchus (P. Oxy. 4133-4300a), Volumes I and II. Philadelphia: American Philosophical Society. I: 267 + II: 396-397

ὥρ κʹ[...

‘2[x] hours’

— P. Oxy. LXI 4172 fr. 2, iii, 1

Genre: mathematical astronomy: parallax table
Provenance: Oxyrhynchus
Date: 4th-5th century CE

Jones, A. (ed.), 1999. Astronomical papyri from Oxyrhynchus (P. Oxy. 4133-4300a), Volumes I and II. Philadelphia: American Philosophical Society. I: 165-166 + II: 150-151

This papyrus is similar in type of content to Ptolemy's Handy Tables.
