TAG₄
râḫu

Akkadian verb

Last edited: 7/15/2024, 7:55:46 AM
Primary meaning remain
Literal translation remain
Variants
TAG₄ râḫu
re-ḫi rēḫi stative m. sg. G
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
remain mathematical expression

(17) ki-i si-man ša₂ GI₆.[DU al-la LA₂ ša₂ GI₆ DIRI LA₂ ša₂ GI₆ TA GI₆ DU NIM] (18) ša₂ TAG₄ ⸢TA 6 LA₂ 1-en⸣ ME TAB

‘(17) If the time by which the night has progressed exceeds the length of night, you deduct the length of night from (the time by which) the night has progressed. (18) What remains you subtract from 6,0. You add 1 day.’

— BM 33451+ (BMAPT No. 61D) obv. 16-17

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-50 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer. 398-399

(37) 1.30 ana KUR šamaš₂ re-ḫi 1.30 ina 6 12 DANNA E₁₁-ma 4.30 IB₂.TAG₄ (38) U₄.28.KAM

‘(37) 1,30 remains until sunrise. You deduct 1,30 from 6,0 (UŠ, i.e.) 12 bēru, 4,30 is the remainder (38) of day 28.’

— BM 32167+ (BMAPT No. 53) rev. ii 37-38

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-250 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer.
