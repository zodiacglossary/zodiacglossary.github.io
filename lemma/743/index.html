DIŠ.U₄ ša₂ SIG
DIŠ.U₄ ša šupli

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 2/3/2025, 9:14:51 AM
Primary meaning rising or setting time of moon's distance below ecliptic
Literal translation (rising) to daylight? of depth
Variants

rising or setting time of moon's distance below ecliptic astronomical quantity

(15') ki-i DIŠ.U₄ ša₂ SIG al-⸢la BE⸣ BAR.NUN u 2 ḪAB-rat DIRI-at BE BAR.NUN u 2 ḪAB-rat (16') TA lib₃-bi-šu₂ LA₂ ana ⸢GI₆ E-bi⸣

‘(15') If the DIŠ.U₄ of depth exceeds the elongation and the ṣiliptu-coefficient and the 2 for the disk you subtract the elongation and the ṣiliptu-coefficient and the 2 for the disk (16') from it. You call it GI₆.’

— MMA 86.11.363 (BMAPT No. 61G) rev. 15'-16'

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-100 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer. 406-407

moon's distance below ecliptic astronomical quantity

(9) KI sin u KI šamaš₂ ša₂ al-la 10 lu-maš DIRI GAM₀ 8 DU KI ⸢BAR⸣.NUN ša₂ NIM u SIG ⸢ša₂⸣-[šu₂] (10) ki TAB TAB ki LA₂ LA₂ ana muḫ-ḫi a-ḫa-miš₂ GAR.GAR-ma ½-šu₂-nu GIŠ-ma GAM₀ DIŠ.U₄ ša₂ [NIM] u S[IG] (11) ša₂ taš-kun DU

‘(9) (The amount) by which the position of Sin and the position of Šamaš exceed 10 (UŠ) of the zodiacal sign you multiply by (0;0),8. You add it with the corresponding ṣiliptu-coefficient for ‘height and depth’ if it is increasing, subtract if it is decreasing. You accumulate (them) together, you compute half of it, and you multiply it by the DIŠ.U₄ of height and depth which you had put down.’

— BM 35399 (BMAPT No. 61A) obv. 9-11

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-50 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer. 395-420
