EN.NUN.U4.ZAL.LA
šāt urri

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 7/3/2023, 10:17:51 AM
Primary meaning Morning watch
Literal translation Morning watch

Morning watch time interval

Third of the three watches of the night.

mul2.GU.LA a-dir 30 ina EN.NUN.U4.ZAL.LA AN.GE6 GAR-ma

‘The Great One is dark: the Moon makes an eclipse in the morning watch.’

— AO 6455 (TCL VI 11 = TU 11) obv. 2

Genre: collection of astrological and astronomical rules
Provenance: Uruk
Date: ca. 200 BCE

Brack-Bernsen, Lis, and Hunger, Hermann. 2002. TU 11. A Collection of Rules for the Prediction of Lunar Phases and of Month Lengths. SCIAMVS 3: 3–90. 7

GE₆ 20 ina ZALAG₂ sin ina IGI MAŠ.MAŠ IGI 2 KUŠ₃ sin 5 1/2 KUŠ₃ ana ULU₃ SIG

‘Night of (day) 20, last part of the night, Sin was 2 cubits in front of the front twin, Sin being 5 1/2 cubits low to the south.’

— BM 32839+ (ADRT III No. -141F) obv. 5'

Genre: astronomical diary
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 142 BCE

Sachs, A. J., Hunger, H. 1996. Astronomical Diaries and Related Texts from Babylonia, Vol. III. Diaries from 164 B.C. to 61 B.C. Vienna, Austria: Verlag der Österreichischen Akademie der Wissenschaften. 124-125
