mꜣꜥ ḥtp

Egyptian noun

Last edited: 8/25/2025, 7:58:48 AM
Primary meaning step
Literal translation Part of setting
Variants
mꜣꜥ ḥtp
Edited by
Andreas Winkler
step (morning last) synodic phenomenon

(n)-ṯꜣy ỉsw 5 šꜥ ḥtr.w 30 r swsw 85 ỉw=f mšꜥ any ỉwt 20 | r mꜣꜥ ḥtp 3 r swsw 1 ỉw=k ỉr 3 r sp 85 r 255

‘From Aries 5° to Gemini 30° makes 85 degrees, proceeding at the rate of 0;20°, as the part of setting, 3 (of them) make 1 degree. You shall multiply 3 times 85, it makes 255.’

— ANAshm.D.O. 525+ 10-11

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Egypt

Ossendrijver, M. and A. Winkler, 2018. Chaldeans on the Nile: Two Egyptian Procedure Texts with Babylonian System A1 and A2 for Mercury, in Scaffolding of Our Thoughts: Essays on Assyriology and the History of Science in Honor of Francesca Rochberg, eds. J. Crisostomo et al. (Leiden: Brill, 2018), 282-418
