MUL-GAL
kakkabu rabû

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 6/2/2024, 4:57:15 PM
Primary meaning Jupiter / meteor
Literal translation large star
Variants
MUL-GAL kakkabu rabû
MUL2-GAL kakkabu rabû
Edited by
Marvin Schreiber
Jupiter planet

[… ana(?) ŠEG3 u IL]LU ME.A GAR-an | […] iti.GU₄ ša₂ MUL₂-GAL 1.12 24 12 MU.AN.NA.MEŠ | [ša2 d.Dil-bat] 16 8 MU.MEŠ ša₂ d.GU₄.UD 46 21 13 MU.MEŠ | [ša2 d.SAG.UŠ … ša2 d.Ṣal-bat-a-nu] 47 MU.MEŠ ša₂ d.UTU 36 54 MU.MEŠ ša₂ d.30 18 MU.MEŠ

‘[If] you (want to) cast a prediction [for…rain and] flood: | […] month II. For Jupiter 72, 24, 12 years, | [for Venus] 16, 8 years, for Mercury 46, 21, 13 years, | [for Saturn …, for Mars] 47 years, for the sun 36, 54 years, for the moon 18 years.’

— AO 6488 (TCL VI 20 = TU 20) rev. 1-4

Genre: astrological weather prediction
Provenance: Uruk
Date: 3rd cenury BCE

Hunger, H. 1976. Astrologische Wettervorhersagen. Zeitschrift fur Assyriologie 66 239

meteor celestial object

MUL₂ GAL ša₂ ma-gal KUR u₃ meš-ḫi TUK-u₂

‘A meteor which was very bright and had a tail.’

— BM 36729 (ADRT I No. -343) rev. 10'

Genre: astronomical diary
Provenance: Babylon
Date: -343 (Artaxerxes III year 15)

Sachs, A. J., Hunger, H. 1988. Astronomical Diaries and Related Texts from Babylonia, Vol. I: Diaries from 652 B.C. to 262 B.C. Vienna: Verlag der Österreichischen Akademie der Wissenschaften. 154
