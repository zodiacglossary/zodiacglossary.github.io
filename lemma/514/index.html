MUL₂ ŠID.MEŠ
kakkabū minâti

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 10/20/2023, 10:03:09 AM
Primary meaning normal stars
Literal translation counting stars
Variants
MUL₂ ŠID.MEŠ kakkabū minâti
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
normal stars group of stars and constellations

reference stars used in astronomical diaries and related texts

DIB.MEŠ ša₂ dil-bat KI MUL₂ ŠID.MEŠ ina 12 MU-ka 4 U₄ ana MU-ka LA₂-ṭi

‘Passages of Venus with the counting stars: in 12 years 4 days are lacking to your year.’

— BM 41004 rev. 5

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon

Brack-Bernsen & Hunger 2005/6, AfO 51, 96-107

(13') 29 24 ME NIM-a šamaš₂ AN.MI A₂ ⸢ULU₃⸣ [u] ⸢MAR⸣ ki-i TAB-[u₂ ...] (14') [dil]-bat GU₄.UD u MUL₂ ŠID.MEŠ IGI.MEŠ MUL₂.BABBAR u AN ša₂ ina bi-ib-lu ina AN.MI-šu₂ IGI.MEŠ [...]

‘(13') (Day) 29, at 24 after sunrise, solar eclipse; when it began on the south and west side, [...] (14') [Ven]us, Mercury, and the counting stars were visible; Jupiter and Mars, which were in their period of invisibility, were visible in its eclipse [...]’

— BM 45745 (ADRT III No. -136B) rev. 13'-14'

Genre: astronomical diary
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 137 BCE

Sachs, A. J., Hunger, H. 1996. Astronomical Diaries and Related Texts from Babylonia, Vol. III. Diaries from 164 B.C. to 61 B.C. Vienna, Austria: Verlag der Österreichischen Akademie der Wissenschaften.
