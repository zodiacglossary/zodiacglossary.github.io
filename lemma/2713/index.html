KI
ašru

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 2/3/2025, 9:09:24 AM
Primary meaning planet being in one of the zodiacal signs of its triplicity
Literal translation place
Variants
KI ašru
Edited by
Alessia Pilloni
place of a planet in a zodiacal signs that belongs to its its triplicity astrological concept, astrological place

only in horoscopes (Pilloni 2024)

MÚL.BABBAR ina 18 PA UŠ KI MÚL.BABBAR GI-át SILIM-⸢át⸣ NÍG.TUKU SUMUN-bar U₄-MEŠ GÍD.DA-MEŠ

‘Jupiter in 18° Sagittarius. The place of Jupiter: (the native’s life[?] will be) prosperous, at peace?; his wealth will be long-lasting, long days (i.e., life).’

— MLC 2190 (BH 10) obv. 6-8

Genre: horoscope
Provenance: Uruk
Date: 3rd century BCE (date of birth in horoscope: S.E. 77 III.4 = -234 June 2/3)

Rochberg, F. 1998. Babylonian Horoscopes. Transactions of the American Philosophical Society, 88, 1–164. 82-85

Pilloni, A. (2024). The Astrological Schemes Behind bīt niṣirtu and KI in the Babylonian Horoscopes. Journal of Ancient Near Eastern History, 11(1), 1-26. https://doi.org/10.1515/janeh-2023-0014
planet assigned to the “Terms” of the month of birth. astrological concept, astrological place

only in horoscopes (Pilloni 2024)

[dele-b]at GU KI dele-bat a-lid˻DUMU˼.MEŠ TUK

‘[Venu]s (in) Aquarius. (The child) was born (in) the “place” of Venus: he will have sons.’

— NCBT 1231 (=BG 9) obv. 6

Genre: horoscope
Provenance: Uruk
Date: Seleucid

Rochberg, F. 1998. Babylonian Horoscopes. Transactions of the American Philosophical Society, 88. 79-81

Pilloni, A. (2024). The Astrological Schemes Behind bīt niṣirtu and KI in the Babylonian Horoscopes. Journal of Ancient Near Eastern History, 11(1), 1-26. https://doi.org/10.1515/janeh-2023-0014
