DIŠ.U₄ ša₂ NIM

Akkadian noun

Primary meaning rising or setting time of moon's distance above ecliptic

rising or setting time of moon's distance above ecliptic astronomical quantity

(12) ki DIŠ.U₄ ša₂ NIM al-la BE u BAR.NUN DIRI-at₂ BE u BAR.NUN TA lib₃-bi [NI]M (13) ana NA GAR-an

‘(12) If the DIS̆.U₄ of height exceeds the elongation and the ṣiliptu-coefficient you subtract the elongation and the ṣiliptu-coefficient from it, (13) you put it down as NA.’

— BM 35399 (BMAPT No. 61A) obv. 12-13

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-50 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer. 398-399

moon's distance above ecliptic astronomical quantity

(9) KI sin u KI šamaš₂ ša₂ al-la 10 lu-maš DIRI GAM₀ 8 DU KI ⸢BAR⸣.NUN ša₂ NIM u SIG ⸢ša₂⸣-[šu₂] (10) ki TAB TAB ki LA₂ LA₂ ana muḫ-ḫi a-ḫa-miš₂ GAR.GAR-ma ½-šu₂-nu GIŠ-ma GAM₀ DIŠ.U₄ ša₂ [NIM] u S[IG] (11) ša₂ taš-kun DU

‘(9) (The amount) by which the position of Sin and the position of Šamaš exceed 10 (UŠ) of the zodiacal sign you multiply by (0;0),8. You add it with the corresponding ṣiliptu-coefficient for ‘height and depth’ if it is increasing, subtract if it is decreasing. You accumulate (them) together, you compute half of it, and you multiply it by the DIŠ.U₄ of height and depth which you had put down.’

— BM 35399 (BMAPT No. 61A) obv. 9-11

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-50 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer.
