δορυφορέω
doruphoreō

Greek verb

Last edited: 7/14/2025, 8:24:56 AM
Primary meaning to body-guard
Literal translation to body-guard
Variants
δορυφορέω doruphorēō
Edited by
Michael Zellmann-Rohrer
to body-guard astrological concept

ὁ τοιοῦτος ἔνδοξος, ἐξουσιαστικός· ἐδορυφορήθη γὰρ ὁ Ἥλιος ὑπὸ τῶν ἀγαθοποιῶν καὶ εὑρέθη ἐπικείμενος τῷ κλήρῳ τῆς τύχης σὺν τῷ οἰκοδεσπότῃ.

‘The one with such (a nativity) is glorious, powerful: for the Sun was body-guarded by the benefic (planets) and was found located in the lot of fortune with the house-master.’

— Vettius Valens 2.27.9

Genre: astrological treatise
Date: 2nd century CE

(GH L 101,III)
