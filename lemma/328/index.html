Akkadian noun

Literal translation Arrow-star

Arrow-star constellation

Approx. corresponding to Canis Maior, Canis Minor, and parts of Puppis and Pyxis

Arrow-star, Sirius star

Sirius (α Canis Maioris)

[iti].ŠU 3.40 mul2.KAK.SI.SA2 2 mul2.UR.A 1 mul2.MUŠ

‘[Mo]nth IV: 3,40 'The Arrow', 2 'The Lion', 1 'The Snake'.’

— BM 34713 (LBAT 1499) obv. 5

Genre: schematic astronomy: 'three stars each' + rising times
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 4th-2nd cent BCE

Horowitz, W., 2014. The Three Stars Each: The Astrolabes and Related Texts (Archiv für Orientforschung, Beiheft 33). Horn/Wien: Berger & Söhne/Institut für Orientalistik der Universität Wien. 126

15 KAK.BAN ŠU₂

‘(Year 39, month II, day) 15, Sirius’ (heliacal) setting.’

— BM 32618 (ADRT VII No. 6) obv. 3'

Genre: astronomical diaries and related texts: normal star almanac
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 274 BCE

Hunger, H. 2014. Astronomical Diaries and Related Texts from Babylonia, Vol. VII: Almanacs and Normal Star Almanacs. Vienna, Austria: Verlag der Österreichischen Akademie der Wissenschaften. 10-11

23 MUL₂.KAK.BAN IGI

‘(Day) 23, Sirius appears.’

— BM 34758 (ADRT VII No. 90) obv. 22

Genre: astronomical diaries and related texts: normal star almanac
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 118 BCE

Hunger, H. 2014. Astronomical Diaries and Related Texts from Babylonia, Vol. VII: Almanacs and Normal Star Almanacs. Vienna, Austria: Verlag der Österreichischen Akademie der Wissenschaften. 208-209
