octotropos

Latin noun

Last edited: 7/29/2024, 9:04:03 AM
Primary meaning octatropos
Literal translation eight-place
Variants
octotropos
Edited by
Kierán Meinhardt
octatropos astrological concept

system of eight astrological places

[cui parti nomen posuit, qui condidit artem, octotropos; per quod stellae diversa volantes quos reddant motus, proprio venit ordine rerum]

‘The founder of astrology gave to this section the title Octotropos; the motions of the planets, which fly through it in the opposite direction, shall follow at the proper place.’

— Manilius, Astronomica 2.968 ff

Genre: astrological treatise

Manilius. Astronomica. Edited and translated by G. P. Goold. Loeb Classical Library 469. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press, 1977. 158-159

Manilius does not use this system. The verses have likely been interpolated, cf. Goold 1977, p. lxi f.
