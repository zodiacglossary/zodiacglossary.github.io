orbis lacteus

Latin noun

Last edited: 4/16/2024, 12:40:33 PM
Primary meaning milky way
Literal translation milky ring
Variants
orbis lacteus
lacteus orbis
Edited by
Kierán Meinhardt
milky way astronomical term

quae ratio orbem lacteum faciat

‘why it causes the milky way [to exist]’

— Firmicus Maternus, Mathesis 1.0.5

Genre: astrological compendium
Provenance: Sicily
Date: ca. 334 CE

Firmicus Maternus. 1897. Ivlii Firmici Materni Matheseos Libri VIII, ed. W. Kroll, F. Skutsch. Stuttgart: B. G. Teubner. 1.3

e niveo lactis fluxisse liquorem pectore reginae divum caelumque colore infecisse suo; quapropter lacteus orbis dicitur,

‘from the snow-white breast of heaven’s queen there flowed a stream of milk which left its colour upon the skies; wherefore is it called the Milky Way’

— Manilius, Astronomica 1.751 ff

Genre: astrological treatise

Manilius. Astronomica. Edited and translated by G. P. Goold. Loeb Classical Library 469. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press, 1977. 64-65
