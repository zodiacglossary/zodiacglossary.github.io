collum scorpii

Latin noun

Last edited: 12/12/2023, 11:34:27 AM
Primary meaning the neck of the Scorpion
Literal translation neck of the scorpion
Variants
collum scorpii
scorpii collum
Edited by
Kierán Meinhardt
the neck of the Scorpion animal part

8°–10° Scorpio

sequentes tres in collo per ordinem constituuntur

‘the following three [degrees] are located in order in the neck [of the Scorpion]’

— Firmicus Maternus, Mathesis 8.4.8

Genre: astrological compendium
Provenance: Sicily
Date: ca. 334 CE

Firmicus Maternus. 1897. Ivlii Firmici Materni Matheseos Libri VIII, ed. W. Kroll, F. Skutsch. Stuttgart: B. G. Teubner. 2.291

See also

〈collum cancri〉 ‘the neck of the Crab’

〈scorpius〉 ‘Scorpio’

〈collum piscis〉 ‘the neck of the Fish’
