TUR₃
tarbaṣu

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 6/26/2023, 6:40:18 PM
Primary meaning halo
Literal translation cattle pen
Variants
TUR₃ tarbaṣu
Edited by
Alessia Pilloni
Mathieu Ossendrijver
halo astronomical term

around moon or sun, attested in astronomical diaries and omen texts

21 GENNA ana ME E-a DIR NU PAP AN.BAR₇ šamaš₂ TUR₃ NIGIN₂ ma-diš iq-tur₇

‘(Day) 21, Saturn’s rising to daylight; clouds, I did not watch. Middle part of the day, the sun was surrounded by a cattle pen, it billowed very much.’

— BM 34651+ (ADRT I No. -342A) obv. 12

Genre: astronomical diary
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 343 BCE

Sachs, A. J., Hunger, H. 1988. Astronomical Diaries and Related Texts from Babylonia, Vol. I: Diaries from 652 B.C. to 262 B.C. Vienna: Verlag der Österreichischen Akademie der Wissenschaften. 158-159

sin TUR3 NU KAD2 NIGIN

‘The moon was surrounded by a halo that was not closed.’

— BM 45850 A 12'

Genre: astronomical diary
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 125 BCE

Sachs, A. J., Hunger, H. 1996. Astronomical Diaries and Related Texts from Babylonia, Vol. III. Diaries from 164 B.C. to 61 B.C. Vienna, Austria: Verlag der Österreichischen Akademie der Wissenschaften. 264-265

30 ina še-ri3 šamaš2 TUR3 NIGIN2

‘The 30th, in the morning, the sun was surrounded by a halo.’

— BM 46229 (ADRT I No. -346) 12

Genre: astronomical diary
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 347 BCE

Sachs, A. J., Hunger, H. 1988. Astronomical Diaries and Related Texts from Babylonia, Vol. I: Diaries from 652 B.C. to 262 B.C. Vienna: Verlag der Österreichischen Akademie der Wissenschaften. 144-145

pen, enclosure, courtyard
(a part of the liver) extispicy
(an ornament)

from OB on
