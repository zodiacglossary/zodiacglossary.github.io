GAR.GAR
kamāru

Akkadian verb

Last edited: 3/22/2024, 3:03:27 PM
Primary meaning to accumulate
Literal translation to heap up
Variants
GAR.GAR kamāru
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
to accumulate mathematical operation, arithmetical operation, additive operation

additive operation used for non-identity conserving addition of at least two quantities that are in some sense symmetrical

(6) KI sin NIM KI sin u KI šamaš₂ ša₂ al-la 10 lu-maš DIRI GAM0 16 DU KI BAR.NUN ša₂ l[u-maš] (7) ša₂-šu₂ ki TAB TAB ki LA₂ LA₂ ana muḫ-ḫi a-ḫa-miš₂ GAR.GAR-ma ½-šu₂-nu GIŠ-ma ana BAR.NUN ⸢GAR⸣-[an]

‘(6) The position of the Moon and the position of the Sun: (the amount) by which they exceed 10 of the zodiacal sign you multiply by (0;0),16. You add it with the corresponding ṣiliptu-coefficient for the zod[iac] if it is increasing, subtract if it is decreasing. You accumulate (them) together, compute half of it and put it down as the ṣiliptu-coefficient.’

— BM 35399 (BMAPT No. 61A) obv. 6-7

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-50 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer. 397-398
