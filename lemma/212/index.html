ZI
nisḫu

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 3/22/2024, 3:09:54 PM
Primary meaning subtraction
Literal translation that which is torn out
Variants
ZI nisḫu
nis-ḫu nisḫu
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
subtraction mathematical quantity, arithmetical quantity, subtractive quantity

(1) 1 ḪUN mi-⸢nu-u₂⸣ ki al 12 ḪUN LA₂-u₂ 11 LA₂ (2) 11 A.RA₂ 30 5.30 5.30 TA 45 (3) ZI ša₂ ḪUN ZI-ma 39.30

‘(1) 1 Aries: whatever it is less than 12 Aries, it is less by 11. (2) 11 times (0);30 is 5;30. You subtract (‘tear out’) 5;30 from 45, (3) the subtraction for Aries, it is 39;30.’

— BM 36722 (BMAPT No. 52) Rev. i 1-3

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-250 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer.

(31) ma-la ina 18-ka kun-nu u ṭe-pi KI-šu₂ la ṭe-pu-u₂ u₃ nis-ḫu TA ŠA₃ ZI-ma al-la ITI (32) ša₂ UGU-šu₂ LA₂-u₂ ana kun-nu E

‘(31) Whatever (month) in your (year) 18 (preceding it) is full, and to which there is no addition added, and a subtraction is subtracted from it, and which is (32) less than the month preceding it, you declare it as full.’

— AO 6455 (TU 11) obv. 31-32

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Uruk
Date: ca. 200 BCE

Brack-Bernsen, Lis, and Hunger, Hermann. 2002. TU 11. A Collection of Rules for the Prediction of Lunar Phases and of Month Lengths. SCIAMVS 3: 3–90.
