Ἀρκτοῦρος
Arktouros

Greek noun

Last edited: 2/26/2025, 2:12:35 PM
Primary meaning Arcturus (α Boötis)
Literal translation Arcturus
Variants
Ἀρκτοῦρος Arktouros
Edited by
Thomas Peeters
Kierán Meinhardt
Arcturus (α Boötis) star, part of constellation

Ὁ μὲν γὰρ ἀνὰ μέσον τῶν σκελῶν τοῦ Ἀρκτοφύλακος κείμενος ἐπίσημος ἀστὴρ Ἀρκτοῦρος ὀνομάζεται.

‘The notable star lying in the midst of the legs of the Bear Keeper is named Arcturus.’

— Geminus 3.9

Genre: astronomy
Date: 1st century BCE

Geminus. 1975. Géminos, Introduction aux Phénomènes, ed. G. Aujac. Paris: Les Belles Lettres. 19

α. ἀρχαίης ὦ θινὸς ἐπεστηλωμένον ἄχθος, εἴποις ὅντιν’ ἔχεις, ἢ τίνος, ἢ ποδαπόν. β. Φίντων’ Ἑρμιονῆα Βαθυκλέος, ὃν πολὺ κῦμα ὤλεσεν, Ἀρκτούρου λαίλαπι χρησάμενον.

‘A. “O stone standing a burden on the ancient beach, tell me whom thou holdest, whose son and whence.” B. “Phinto the son of Bathycles of Hermione, who perished in the heavy sea, encountering the blast of Arcturus.”’

— Leonidas of Tarentum

Genre: epigram
Provenance: Hermione

Anth. Gr. 7.503
