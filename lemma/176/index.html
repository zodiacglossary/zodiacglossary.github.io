κακόω
kakoō

Greek verb

Last edited: 7/11/2023, 12:22:49 PM
Primary meaning to oppress
Literal translation to oppress
Variants
κακόω kakoō
Edited by
Michael Zellmann-Rohrer
to oppress verb

κακοῦται ἀστὴρ ὅταν μὴ εἰς ἁρμόζοντα τύχῃ παρὼν ἢ περιέχηται ὑπὸ κακοποιῶν ἢ κατ’ ἐναντίον ἢ ἐπιδέκατον· ὅτι ἀγαθοποιὰ ζῴδιά εἰσιν ὁ ὡροσκόπος, τὸ μεσουράνημα, ιαʹ, εʹ θεός, θεά· κακοποιὰ δὲ βʹ, δʹ ϛʹ, ζʹ, ⟨ηʹ⟩, ιβʹ

‘A planet is oppressed whenever it is present in an unsuitable (place?) or is surrounded by malefics or in a relation of opposition (to them) or being “tithed” (by them): benefic zodiac signs are the Ascendant, Midheaven, the 11th (place), the 5th, God, Goddess, and malefic ones are the 2nd, 4th, 6th, 7th, <8th,> 12th (places).’

— Sarapion of Alexandria, Various Names for Configurations of the Stars

Genre: astrology

CCAG VIII.4 226
