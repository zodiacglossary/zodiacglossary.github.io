KUR šamaš2
napāḫ Šamaš

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 7/15/2024, 7:27:22 AM
Primary meaning time until sunrise
Literal translation rising of Šamaš
Variants
KUR šamaš2 napāḫ Šamaš
KUR ? abbreviation of KUR šamaš2

Edited by

time until sunrise time interval

time interval from a moment after midnight until sunrise. A rare synonym is mūšu ana namāri.

29 1.36 KUR šamaš2

‘(day) 29, 1,36 (UŠ before) sunrise’

— BM 45849+ (ACT No. 123a) obv. 11 col. M (= time of New Moon)

Genre: mathematical astronomy: synodic table
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 2nd century BCE

Neugebauer ACT

(36) 6 DANNA LA₂ ša₂ ME ⸢ša₂⸣ ITI.ŠE U₄.28.KAM 3 6 DANNA ina 4.30 9 DANNA (37) E₁₁ 1.30 ana KUR šamaš₂ re-ḫi 1.30 ina 6 12 DANNA E₁₁-ma 4.30 IB₂.TAG₄ (38) U₄.28.KAM 4.30 ⸢ME⸣ NIM.A ana KUR E.A GAR-an

‘(36) The length of daylight for the 28th of month XII is 6 beru. You deduct 3,0 (UŠ i.e.) 6 bēru, from 4,30 (UŠ , i.e.) 9 bēru , (37) 1,30 (UŠ) until sunrise remains. You deduct 1,30 (UŠ) from 6,0 (UŠ, i.e.) 12 bēru, 4,30 is the remainder (38) for day 28. You put down 4,30 (UŠ) as the time (from New Moon) to sunrise in order to predict KUR.’

— BM 32167+ (BMAPT No. 53) rev. ii 35-38

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-250 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer.
