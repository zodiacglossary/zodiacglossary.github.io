GURUN
inbu

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 6/3/2024, 7:45:35 AM
Primary meaning moon
Literal translation fruit
Variants
GURUN inbu
Edited by
Marvin Schreiber
fruit common noun
moon an epithet of the moon(god)

rare in astronomical and astrological texts, common in hymns

UD-7-[KAM AGA ma-a]š2-la BAR BA3 BA3 za-a-zu BA3 pa-r[a-su] | BA3 [ba-an-t]u BA3 mi-šil BA3 30 mi-šil miš2-[li] | 30 [A.RA2 0;30] 15 15 A.RA2 4 60 60 d.A-nu3 im-bi GURUN E[N ITI]

‘The 7th [day: a half] crown, BAR(half): BA3(30). BA3: to divide, BA3: to cut, | BA3: share, BA3: half. Half(BA3) of the moon(30) is half of a half. | 0;30 [x 0;30] = 0;15 15 x 4 = 60 60 (is) Anu, he called the “fruit”, lo[rd of the month].’

— K 2164+ 11-13

Genre: mystical-explanatory text
Provenance: Nineveh
Date: 7th cent BCE

Livingstone, A. 1986. Mystical and mythological explanatory works of Assyrian and Babylonian scholars. Oxford: Clarendon Press. 22
