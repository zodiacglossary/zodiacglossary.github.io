stationem solvo

Latin verb

Last edited: 9/23/2024, 9:22:53 AM
Primary meaning to cease being stationary
Literal translation to solve a station
Variants
stationem solvo
solvo stationem
Edited by
Kierán Meinhardt
to cease being stationary astronomical event, synodic phenomenon

Turn yero in omnibus proficiet, cum Saturnus primam stationem in Leone vel in Capricorno solverit.

‘Everything will go forward when Saturn moves from his first station in Leo or in Capricorn.’

— Firmicus Maternus, Mathesis 5.3.10

Genre: astrological compendium
Provenance: Sicily
Date: ca. 334 CE

Firmicus Maternus. 1897. Ivlii Firmici Materni Matheseos Libri VIII, ed. W. Kroll, F. Skutsch. Stuttgart: B. G. Teubner. 2.31
