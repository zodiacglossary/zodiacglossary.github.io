SAG.ME.GAR

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 6/17/2024, 8:00:31 AM
Primary meaning Jupiter
Literal translation ?
Variants
d.SAG.ME.GAR

Edited by
Marvin Schreiber
Jupiter planet

aš2-ša2 UD-1-KAM ša2 iti.BAR d.SAG3.ME.GAR KI 30 ŠU2-u2 UD-12 AN.GE6 30 GAR-nu 3.20 ša2 ina EŠ2.GAR3 iq-bu-u2 ana UD-20-KAM 3.20 UG7

‘Because Jupiter on the 1st day of month I set with the Moon, (and) on the 12th day the Moon made an eclipse, the king, as it is said in the series, on the 20th the king will die.’

— AO 6455 (TCL VI 11 = TU 11) obv. 14

Genre: collection of astrological and astronomical rules
Provenance: Uruk
Date: ca. 200 BCE

Brack-Bernsen, Lis, and Hunger, Hermann. 2002. TU 11. A Collection of Rules for the Prediction of Lunar Phases and of Month Lengths. SCIAMVS 3: 3–90. 8

[DIŠ 30 ina mul.UR.GU.LA AN.GE6] ⌈GAR⌉-ma EN.NUN ig-mur u IM SI.SA2 DU ina AN.GE₆-šu2 d.SAG.ME.GAR NU GUB mul.UDU.IDIM.SAG.UŠ u2-lu d.ṣal-bat-a-nu ina mul.lu2.ḪUN.GA2 u2-⌈lu⌉ [ina mul.PA.BIL.SAG] | [u2-lu ina] ⌈mul.AŠ.IKU⌉ GUB-iz KI.MIN ina AN.GE6-šu2 TUR3 NIGIN-ma mul.LUGAL ina ŠA3-šu2 GUB-iz

‘[If an eclipse] occurs [when the moon is in Leo], and the first watch finished and the north wind blows, in its eclipse Jupiter does not stand, Saturn or Mars stand in Aries or [Sagittarius] | [or in] Pisces; Ditto: during its eclipse the moon is surrounded by a lunar halo and the King-star stands in it.’

— VAT 7847+AO 6448 obv. 1-2

Genre: microzodiac text
Provenance: Uruk
Date: early 2nd cent BCE

Weidner, E., 1967. Gestirn-Darstellungen auf babylonischen Tontafeln. Wien: Hermann Böhlaus Nachf. 15
