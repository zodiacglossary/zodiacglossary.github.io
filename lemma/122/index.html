NIM
šūqu

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 11/12/2023, 5:11:02 PM
Primary meaning distance above ecliptic
Literal translation height
Variants
NIM šūqu
LA₂ šūqu
šu-qa šūqu
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
distance above ecliptic astronomical quantity

NIM u SIG ša₂ sin GAR

‘You put down the height and depth of Sin.’

— BM 55530 (BMAPT No. 61C) rev. 5

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-50 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer

šu-qa u šup-lu ša₂ ᵈsin KIN.KIN-ma

‘The 'height' and 'depth' of Sin you investigate, and (...)’

— BM 35325 (LBAT 1600) rev. 9'

Genre: astrology
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 450-50 BCE

Sachs, Abraham, Strassmaier, Johann N., Pinches, Theophilus. G. (1955). Late Babylonian astronomical and related texts.

maximum mathematical quantity

(3) ša₂ GENNA 25.24.5 NIM ⸢2⸣[2.41.25 SIG] (4) 12 taš an-na-a [ša₂ ME.MEŠ]

‘(3) For Saturn: the ‘height’ is 25;24,5, [the ‘depth’ is 22;41,25], (4) the difference is (0);12; thus [for the ‘days’].’

— A 3418 (BMAPT No. 41) rev. 3-4

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Uruk
Date: ca. 170-200 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer 310

(5) ša₂ GENNA 14.4.42.30 LA₂ 11.14.2.⸢30⸣ [SIG] (6) 12 taš an-na-a KI.⸢MEŠ⸣

‘(5) For Saturn: the ‘height’ is 14;4,42,30, [the ‘depth’ is] 11;14,2, 30 , (6) the difference is (0);12; thus the positions.’

— A 3418 (BMAPT No. 41) rev. 5-6
