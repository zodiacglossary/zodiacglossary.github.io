Middle watch of the night time interval

Second of the three watches of the night.

mul2.SIM.MAḪ a-dir 30 ina EN.NUN.MURUB4.BA AN.GE6 GAR-ma

‘The Swallow is dark: the Moon makes an eclipse in the middle watch.’

— AO 6455 (TCL VI 11 = TU 11) obv. 1

Genre: collection of astrological and astronomical rules
Provenance: Uruk
Date: ca. 200 BCE

Brack-Bernsen, Lis, and Hunger, Hermann. 2002. TU 11. A Collection of Rules for the Prediction of Lunar Phases and of Month Lengths. SCIAMVS 3: 3–90. 7

GE₆ 12 MURUB₄ sin TUR₃ NIGIN₂ AN ⸢ina⸣ ŠA₃ GUB

‘Night of (day) 12, middle part of the night, Sin was surrounded by a halo, Mars stood in it.’

— BM 35195 (ADRT I No. -378) obv. 5'

Genre: astronomical diary
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 379 BCE

Sachs, A. J., Hunger, H. 1988. Astronomical Diaries and Related Texts from Babylonia, Vol. I: Diaries from 652 B.C. to 262 B.C. Vienna: Verlag der Österreichischen Akademie der Wissenschaften. 90-91

MU.1.ME.58.KAM | iti.GAN GE6 27 ina MURUB4-ti3 | lu2.TUR a-lid

‘Year 158 (S.E.), | month IX, night of the 27th, in the middle watch, | the child was born.’

— BM 34567 (BH 32) rev. 1-3

Genre: horoscope
Provenance: Babylon
Date: 2nd century

Rochberg, F. 1998. Babylonian Horoscopes. Transactions of the American Philosophical Society, 88. 147
