ŠU.SI
ubānu

Akkadian noun

Last edited: 11/3/2024, 3:14:04 PM
Primary meaning finger
Literal translation finger
Variants
ŠU.SI ubānu
SI ubānu
u ubānu
Edited by
Mathieu Ossendrijver
finger unit of celestial position

for distance to ecliptic or measure of eclipse magnitude

⸢ana⸣ tar-ṣa 1 ŠU.SI ḪAB-rat ḫe-pi-ti 1.⸢38.24⸣ [GAR-an]

‘Opposite 1 finger of the "broken disk" [you put down] 1,38;24 (barleycorns).’

— BM 34079 (BMAPT No. 65) obv. 20'

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-50 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer. 426-427

a-na 1 DANNA 8 u NIM u SIG

‘For 1 bēru it (Jupiter) ascends and descends 8 fingers.’

— BM 37266 (BMAPT No. 47) obv. 4

Genre: mathematical astronomy: procedure text
Provenance: Babylon
Date: ca. 350-250 BCE

Ossendrijver, M. 2012. Babylonian Mathematical Astronomy: Procedure Texts. New York: Springer. 334

finger unit of length
