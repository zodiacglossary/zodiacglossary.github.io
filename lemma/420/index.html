συνδύνω
sundunō

Greek verb

Last edited: 6/10/2024, 7:43:28 AM
Primary meaning to set simultaneously
Literal translation to sink
Variants
συνδύνω sundunō
συνδύομαι sunduomai
Edited by
Thomas Peeters
to set simultaneously astronomical event

Εἰσί δὲ δύσεις διτταί· οἵ τε γὰρ ἀφανισμοὶ δύσεις εἰσί· τοῦτο δέ ἐστιν ὅταν ἅμα συνδύνῃ τῷ ἡλίῳ τὸ ἄστρον, καὶ ὅταν ἀνατέλλοντος δύνῃ.

‘And the settings are twofold; for the disappearances are settings; and this happens when the star sets simultaneously with the sun, and when it sets during the rising [of the sun].’

— Theophrastus, On Weather Signs 1.2

Genre: meteorological treatise
Date: 4th/3rd century BCE

Theophrastus. 1916. Enquiry into Plants, Volume II: Books 6-9. On Odours. Weather Signs. Translated by Arthur F. Hort, ed. A.F. Hort. Loeb Classical Library 79. Cambridge Massachusetts: Harvard University Press. 390
